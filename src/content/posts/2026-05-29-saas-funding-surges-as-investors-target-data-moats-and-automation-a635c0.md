## Investors Bet on Data-Rich SaaS

H1 closed a $40 million round led by CVS Health, citing its unique doctor-level data set. This investment is significant because it shows that investors are willing to back SaaS startups that have developed defensible data advantages. Finly raised a multi-investor round backed by Social Capital, AngelList, Gemba Capital, and Omphalos Ventures. Finly automates accounts-payable processes for mid- and large-sized businesses. Its funding includes investments from 91springboard via the Atal Innovation Mission and Niti Aayog.

The funding rounds for H1 and Finly highlight a pattern: investors are chasing vertical-specific SaaS with recurring revenue and defensible data or workflow advantages. This trend is driven by the increasing importance of data in business decision-making and the need for automation in various industries.

## The Rise of Vertical-Specific SaaS

The deals illustrate a shift in funding priorities. Investors are now focusing on SaaS startups that have developed unique data sets or workflow advantages. This shift is driven by the increasing competition in the SaaS market and the need for startups to differentiate themselves.

Seekify Technologies raised $1.5 million from Sequoia Capital India's Surge program, while Slintel secured a $1.5 million seed round led by Stellaris Venture Partners. Tookitaki closed a $19.2 million Series A backed by Viola Fintech and SIG Asia Investment. These funding rounds demonstrate that investors are actively looking for SaaS startups with innovative solutions and scalable business models.

## New Financing Models Emerge

The emergence of new financing models is also a significant trend in the SaaS funding landscape. Indie.vc offers revenue-share financing ranging from $100k to $1 million, with founders repaying 3-7% of monthly revenue until the fund is repaid three times the original investment. This financing model provides an alternative to traditional venture capital and can be attractive to SaaS startups that want to maintain control and flexibility.

The SaaS funding landscape has evolved significantly over the years. In the early days of SaaS, investors were focused on funding startups that developed horizontal SaaS solutions. However, as the market has matured, investors have shifted their focus to vertical-specific SaaS solutions.
