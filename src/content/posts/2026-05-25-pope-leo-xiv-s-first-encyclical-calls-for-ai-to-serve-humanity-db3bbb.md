Pope Leo XIV's first encyclical, *Magnifica Humanitas*, released on May 15, 2026, emphasizes that AI must serve humanity. The 42,300-word document highlights four key dangers: the manipulation of education, threats to child safety, the concentration of algorithmic control, and the development of autonomous weapons. Pope Leo XIV frames these risks as moral failures that could undermine human dignity.

## The encyclical's core message
*Magnifica Humanitas* adapts the Vatican's moral framework to the digital age, invoking the concept of *humanitas* – the innate dignity of every person – as the benchmark for any AI system. The Pope calls for transparency, accountability, and a clear chain of responsibility.

## Vatican's moral framework for technology
The Vatican has a long tradition of evaluating technology through a moral lens. Pope Leo XIV's encyclical adds a new layer of moral oversight, emphasizing the need for AI to serve humanity, not just the powerful few.

## The Pope's appeal for solidarity
The encyclical is a call for solidarity, emphasizing the importance of working together to ensure that AI serves the common good. Pope Leo XIV's message is clear: AI must be developed and used in a way that prioritizes human dignity and well-being.
