# Musk's Lawsuit Against OpenAI Dismissed

Elon Musk has lost his lawsuit against OpenAI and its CEO Sam Altman. A California jury unanimously decided that Musk's lawsuit was filed too late. The lawsuit centered around Musk's claim that he was deceived by Altman into co-founding OpenAI as a nonprofit, only to see it shift to a for-profit company after Musk donated $38 million.

## Background of the Lawsuit

Musk and Altman co-founded OpenAI in 2015, but Musk left in 2018 after being denied control of the company. The lawsuit was filed in federal court, with Musk alleging that Altman and other OpenAI executives manipulated him into investing in the company under false pretenses. Microsoft, which has invested $13 billion in OpenAI, was also accused of helping in the scheme.

## The Trial and Verdict

During the trial, both Musk and Altman took the stand, presenting their sides of the story. Musk claimed that Altman deceived him about OpenAI's nonprofit status and intentions, while Altman testified that Musk backed the idea of OpenAI becoming a for-profit business and wanted control of it for the long run. The jury deliberated for roughly two hours before returning a unanimous verdict in favor of OpenAI. The decision was based on the statute of limitations, which had expired for Musk's claims.
