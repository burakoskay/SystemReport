## Overcoming Regulatory Hurdles

The journey to this breakthrough was not without its challenges. The story of MDMA-assisted psychotherapy for PTSD serves as a prime example of regulatory hurdles that can hinder medical progress. Despite promising results, the DEA's classification of MDMA as a Schedule I drug in 1985 brought research to a grinding halt. It took 15 years of persistent effort from researchers and advocates to persuade the FDA to allow clinical studies.

The impact of this delay cannot be overstated. Thousands of patients who could have benefited from this treatment were denied access, and the lack of research into its therapeutic potential meant that other, less effective treatments continued to be used. The delay also highlights the need for more flexible and adaptive regulatory frameworks that can keep pace with emerging medical research.

## Advances in HIV Prevention

Lenacapavir, a long-acting antiviral injection, has been proven to be highly effective in preventing HIV infection. The treatment, which requires only two injections per year, has demonstrated a 96-100% reduction in risk compared to background rates.

The significance of this breakthrough lies in its potential to overcome adherence challenges associated with daily pills. In regions where access to healthcare is limited and stigma is high, the ability to provide protection with just two injections per year could make a significant impact. This treatment could be particularly effective in areas with high HIV prevalence, where access to healthcare is limited and the stigma surrounding HIV is high.
