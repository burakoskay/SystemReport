## Meta's Smart Glasses Hit Regulatory Roadblock in Europe

Meta is delaying the European launch of its smart glasses, citing EU regulations and supply constraints. The product, developed with EssilorLuxottica, is currently only available in the United States.

## EU Regulations Pose Design Challenges

The European Union requires all devices to have detachable batteries by 2027, which creates design difficulties for compact smart glasses. This regulation will either shorten battery duration or decrease the available space for additional functionalities. The smart glasses depend on artificial intelligence for their operation, but EU regulations will limit their available features.

## Technical Mechanics

The smart glasses developed by Meta and EssilorLuxottica rely on artificial intelligence to operate. AI-powered wearables like smart glasses require complex hardware and software systems to function effectively. The detachable battery requirement imposed by the EU may necessitate changes to the device's design, potentially impacting its performance and user experience.

## History of Smart Glasses Development

Meta has been investing in wearable technology, including smart glasses, as part of its broader AI development strategy. The company has partnered with luxury brands like Ray-Ban and Oakley to develop high-end smart glasses. Despite the current regulatory challenges, Meta remains committed to advancing its wearable technology offerings.

## Regulatory Landscape

The EU's regulations on wearable devices, including smart glasses, aim to ensure consumer safety and protect the environment. The requirement for detachable batteries is part of a broader effort to reduce electronic waste and promote sustainability. However, this regulation poses challenges for device manufacturers, who must balance regulatory compliance with product performance and user experience.

## Industry Implications

The issue exists beyond Meta, as Apple has postponed several software updates in Europe because of regulatory issues. ## Meta Seeks Exemptions for AI-Powered Wearables

Meta is currently in talks with EU officials to seek exemptions for AI-powered wearables and smart glasses technology. The company maintains its commitment to developing wearable devices which serve as a fundamental component of its entire AI development plan.

## What's Next

Meta needs to overcome the regulatory hurdles to release the product with all features operational. The company will likely continue to work with EU officials to find a solution that meets both regulatory requirements and product goals.
