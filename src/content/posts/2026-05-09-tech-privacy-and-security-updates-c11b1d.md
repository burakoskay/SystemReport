## General Motors Agrees to $12.75M Settlement Over Driver Privacy

General Motors has agreed to a $12.75 million settlement with a group of law enforcement agencies led by California Attorney General Rob Bonta over a privacy-related issue. The details of the settlement have not been disclosed.

The settlement comes as tech companies face increased scrutiny over their handling of user data and surveillance. This increased scrutiny is part of a broader shift in how regulators and consumers view the collection and use of personal data by tech companies.

## Banned Drones and Routers to Receive Critical Updates Until 2029

The Federal Communications Commission (FCC) has issued a notice allowing software and firmware updates for banned drones and routers until January 2029. This move aims to ensure that users of these devices can continue to receive critical updates and patches.

The FCC has been actively working to address concerns over the security and privacy implications of certain technology products. This action is part of a broader effort to ensure that tech companies prioritize user safety and security.

## macOS Privacy and Security Settings Deemed Unreliable

A recent demonstration has shown that macOS's Privacy and Security settings can be misleading, allowing apps to access protected folders without explicit permission. 

This issue highlights the ongoing challenges in ensuring the security and privacy of users' data on various platforms. It also underscores the need for tech companies to prioritize transparency and user control when it comes to data collection and use.

## EARN IT Act Sparks Concerns Over Encryption and Surveillance

The Eliminating Abusive and Rampant Neglect of Interactive Technologies Act of 2023 (EARN IT Act) has raised concerns among privacy and security experts. 

The bill aims to combat child sexual abuse material but may undermine encryption and increase surveillance. This has led to a heated debate over the balance between protecting users and preserving individual freedoms.

## GrapheneOS: A Privacy-Focused Mobile Operating System

GrapheneOS is a non-profit open-source project developing a privacy and security-focused mobile operating system with Android app compatibility. 

The OS aims to improve the privacy and security of users' data and devices. This project is part of a broader movement to create alternative, privacy-focused technologies that prioritize user control and data protection.
