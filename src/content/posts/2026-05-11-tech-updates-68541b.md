## Technical Mechanics: How Zero-Day Exploits Work

A zero-day exploit is a flaw in software that hackers discover before the company does. It's called "zero-day" because the company has zero days to fix it before the bad guys start exploiting it. In the case of Apple's recent update, the zero-day exploit could have allowed hackers to access user data, including messages, photos, and contacts.
