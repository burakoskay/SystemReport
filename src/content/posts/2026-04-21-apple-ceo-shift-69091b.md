## Introduction to Change
Apple's CEO, Tim Cook, is stepping down. John Ternus, the company's senior vice president of hardware engineering, will replace him as CEO on September 1. This transition marks a significant shift in Apple's leadership, the first since Cook took over in 2011 following Steve Jobs' passing.

## The Man Behind the Transition
John Ternus has been with Apple for about half his life, joining the company just four years after graduating from the University of Pennsylvania. His work at Apple has included oversight of the hardware engineering teams behind the iPhone, iPad, Mac, Apple Watch, AirPods, and Vision Pro. Ternus' experience and contributions to Apple's product line make him a fitting successor to lead the company into its next phase.

## Historical Context and Precedent
Apple's history is marked by significant transitions, none as pivotal as the handover from Steve Jobs to Tim Cook. When Jobs passed away in 2011, Cook took the reins, leading Apple through a period of immense growth and innovation. The company's market capitalization grew from under $350 billion to over $4 trillion during Cook's tenure. The introduction of the Apple Watch, AirPods, and expansion into subscription services have been key factors in this growth.

## Looking to the Future
