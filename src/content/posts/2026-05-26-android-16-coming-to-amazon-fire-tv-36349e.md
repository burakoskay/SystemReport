## Android 16 Lands on Amazon Fire TV

Amazon is bringing Android 16 to its Fire TV lineup, developer documentation confirms. This move comes as the company is building out VegaOS, a new base for Fire TV products. The decision to update existing Fire TV devices with the latest Android version indicates Amazon's commitment to supporting its existing user base.

## Details on the Rollout

The documentation doesn't provide a specific timeline for the rollout or details on which Fire TV devices will be eligible for the update. However, it does indicate that Amazon is committed to supporting its existing user base with the latest Android versions. This is significant because it shows that Amazon is prioritizing its existing customers, even as it invests in the development of VegaOS.

## Context: Fire TV and VegaOS

Amazon's Fire TV operating system is based on Android, and the company has been working on a new operating system called VegaOS. VegaOS is designed to provide a more streamlined and secure experience for Fire TV users. However, the company's focus on VegaOS doesn't mean it's abandoning its existing Android-based Fire TV devices. Instead, it appears that Amazon is taking a dual approach, with VegaOS serving as a foundation for new devices and Android 16 being made available for existing devices.
