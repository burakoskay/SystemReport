## Instagram's Feed Update Sparks Outrage

Instagram has updated its feed to use a side-scrolling format, similar to its stories feature. The update has been met with widespread outrage from users, who are frustrated with the change. The new format allows users to tap through posts, rather than scrolling up and down.

The update appears to be an attempt to mimic the success of Instagram's stories feature, which has become incredibly popular. However, users are not happy with the change, and many are calling for the old format to be reinstated. It's unclear if Instagram will reverse the change or if users will eventually adapt to the new format.

## The History of Instagram's Feed

Instagram's feed has undergone several changes since its inception. Initially, the feed was a simple, chronological list of posts from users you followed. However, over time, Instagram has introduced new features, such as the algorithm-driven feed and the stories feature.

The algorithm-driven feed, which was introduced in 2016, uses machine learning to prioritize posts that are most likely to engage you. The stories feature, which was introduced in 2017, allows users to share photos and videos that disappear after 24 hours.
