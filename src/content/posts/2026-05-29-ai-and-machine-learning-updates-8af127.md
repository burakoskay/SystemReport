## Controversy Surrounds Amazon's AI Animated Series

Amazon's decision to produce an AI-animated series based on the popular character 'Good Advice Cupcake' has sparked controversy. The original creator, Loryn Brantz, expressed her discontent on social media, revealing that she was not consulted or compensated for the project. According to a report by The Verge, Brantz created The Good Advice Cupcake for BuzzFeed years ago, and the company licensed the character for a new Amazon series—made with AI—without her consent.

The show is the latest example of AI-generated content being used in entertainment. While AI animation has the potential to transform the industry, it also raises concerns about ownership and creative rights. This incident highlights the need for clearer guidelines and regulations regarding AI-generated content.

## EFF Criticizes AI Patents

The Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF) has criticized a patent granted to Hampton Creek, a San Francisco-based food-tech company. The patent, US Patent No. 9,760,834, describes a 'machine-learning enabled discovery platform' for discovering new ingredients. EFF lawyer Daniel Nazer argues that the patent 'reads like the table of contents of an intro to AI textbook' and covers basic machine-learning techniques.

The EFF's criticism highlights the ongoing debate about patent reform and the need for more nuanced approaches to intellectual property. The organization argues that such patents can stifle innovation and hinder progress in the field of AI and machine learning.
