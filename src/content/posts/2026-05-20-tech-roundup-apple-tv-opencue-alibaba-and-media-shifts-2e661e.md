## Apple TV's New Series and F1 Ambitions

Apple TV has premiered its newest thriller-comedy series, 'Maximum Pleasure Guaranteed,' with the first two episodes now available to stream. The show's plot and main cast have not been disclosed, but it joins Apple's growing lineup of original content.

Meanwhile, Apple's global F1 ambitions have hit a roadblock as Sky extends its U.K. and Italy rights. This development may make it more challenging for Apple to expand its Formula One deal beyond the U.S.

## OpenCue: An Open-Source Render Manager

Google Cloud and Sony Pictures Imageworks have collaborated to open-source their internal queuing system, Cue 3, now renamed as OpenCue. This high-performance render manager is designed for the visual effects and animation industry, allowing studios to better manage their rendering processes and adapt to today's fast-paced production schedules.

OpenCue's development is a significant step in the media and entertainment industry, as it provides studios with a scalable and secure solution for managing their rendering needs. The system has evolved over 15 years at Sony and has scaled to over 150,000 cores between Sony's on-premise data center and GCP.

## The Media and Entertainment Industry's Shift to Cloud-Based Rendering

The media and entertainment industry's adoption of cloud-based rendering solutions like OpenCue marks a significant shift towards leveraging cloud technology to manage complex rendering processes. This trend is driven by the increasing demand for high-quality content and the need for studios to adapt to fast-paced production schedules.

Cloud-based rendering solutions offer several benefits, including scalability, security, and cost-effectiveness. By leveraging cloud technology, studios can reduce their on-premise infrastructure costs and increase their rendering capacity. Additionally, cloud-based solutions enable studios to collaborate more easily with remote teams and freelancers.

The use of cloud-based rendering solutions also enables studios to take advantage of advanced technologies such as artificial intelligence and machine learning. For instance, Google Cloud's Celebrity Recognition feature uses AI to recognize celebrities in video content, making it easier for creators and platforms to organize their content and cater to personalized experiences.

## A History of Open-Source Render Managers

The development of OpenCue is not an isolated incident. There have been other open-source render managers in the past, such as OpenShadingLanguage and OpenFX. These projects aimed to provide studios with an open-source alternative to proprietary rendering solutions.

The media and entertainment industry has a history of adopting open-source technologies to drive innovation and reduce costs. The use of open-source software has become increasingly prevalent in the industry, with many studios and production companies leveraging open-source solutions to manage their rendering needs.

## Alibaba's Restructuring

Alibaba Group Holding is splitting into six groups, including a digital media and entertainment business. The new organizational structure aims to empower each business to become more agile and enhance decision-making.

The digital media and entertainment group will include film studio Alibaba Pictures, video streamer Youku, and other businesses. This restructuring may have significant implications for the media and entertainment industry, as Alibaba seeks to unlock the value of its various businesses.

## Shifts in the Media and Entertainment Industry

The media and entertainment industry is undergoing significant shifts, with companies adapting to new technologies and changing consumer behaviors. The COVID-19 pandemic has accelerated the trend towards remote work, with many companies adopting flexible work arrangements.

The use of blockchain technology is also expected to make waves in the media and entertainment industry. Blockchain-based platforms have the potential to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of the industry's value chain, particularly in areas such as funding creative projects, digital advertising, and piracy prevention.
