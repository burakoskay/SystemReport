## Nuro Clears Hurdle for Driverless Testing

Nuro, a Silicon Valley autonomous vehicle (AV) startup, has secured a permit to test its driverless robotaxis on California roads. This development comes ahead of Uber's anticipated launch of its own robotaxi service.

The permit, granted by the California Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV), allows Nuro to begin testing its vehicles without a human driver present. While Nuro has not yet started driverless testing, the permit brings the company one step closer to deploying its autonomous technology on public roads.

## The Road to Driverless Testing

Nuro's permit is a significant milestone for the company, which has been working on its autonomous technology for several years. The company's robotaxis are designed to operate without human intervention, using a combination of sensors, including lidar, radar, and cameras, to navigate roads.

According to reports, Uber and Nuro plan to start fully autonomous testing later this year. Uber's robotaxi service is expected to launch soon, with the company already conducting testing in various cities.
