The fast food industry is undergoing a significant transformation with the introduction of AI-powered chatbots in drive-thrus. Dairy Queen is the latest chain to adopt this technology, aiming to speed up service and increase sales. The chatbot, built by Presto, will be launched in dozens of locations across the US and Canada. 
      
      This move is part of a larger trend of companies embracing AI to enhance customer experience and improve operational efficiency. However, it also raises questions about job displacement and the potential for human workers to be replaced by machines. 
      
      In other tech news, Intel has refreshed its non-Ultra Core CPUs with new silicon, marking a significant update to its product line. This move is expected to bring improved performance and power efficiency to a wider range of devices. Meanwhile, Amazon has announced that it will no longer release Fire Sticks that support sideloading, a decision that may have implications for users who rely on third-party apps. 
      
      As technology continues to advance and permeate various aspects of our lives, it's essential to consider the potential consequences and benefits of these developments. From AI-powered chatbots in drive-thrus to updates in computing hardware, the future of tech is shaping up to be exciting and unpredictable.
