## Airbnb's AI writes most new code

Airbnb reports that AI now writes 60 % of the code for its newest features. The AI‑powered support bot resolves 40 % of customer tickets without human escalation. This shift towards AI-generated code indicates a significant change in the company's development process. By automating a substantial portion of its coding, Airbnb can accelerate the deployment of new features and improve the efficiency of its development team.

The use of AI in software development is becoming increasingly prevalent, with many companies exploring the potential benefits of machine learning algorithms in coding. Airbnb's adoption of AI-generated code highlights the growing importance of automation in the tech industry.

The AI-powered support bot is also a key aspect of Airbnb's customer service strategy. By resolving 40 % of customer tickets without human escalation, the company can reduce the workload of its customer support team and focus on more complex issues. This can lead to improved customer satisfaction and reduced support costs.

## Blackmagic camera pairs with Apple Watch

Blackmagic released a prototype that accepts Apple Watch commands for remote operation. The camera records Apple ProRes video at up to 4K resolution. This innovative product enables creators to control their cameras remotely using their Apple Watch, providing a new level of flexibility and convenience.

The integration of Apple Watch control with Blackmagic's camera technology demonstrates the trend towards wearable devices becoming more integral to creative workflows. As wearable technology continues to advance, we can expect to see more innovative applications in the tech industry.

The use of Apple Watch as a remote control also highlights the potential for wearable devices to simplify complex tasks. By providing a simple and intuitive interface, creators can focus on capturing high-quality content rather than worrying about camera settings.
