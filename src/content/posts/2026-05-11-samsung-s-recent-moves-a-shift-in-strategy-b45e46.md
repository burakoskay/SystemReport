## Samsung Rolls Out Stable One UI 8.5 Update After Five Months in Beta

Samsung has finally started rolling out the stable version of One UI 8.5, a software update that was in beta for over five months. This update is significant, as it shows Samsung's ability to improve its software update process. However, it also highlights the company's inconsistent approach to software updates, with some devices receiving updates quickly and others taking much longer.

According to 9to5Google, Samsung has gotten faster at software updates in recent years, but One UI 8.5 is an odd case. The update was initially released in beta in January, but it's only now being rolled out to the general public. This delay may be due to various factors, including testing and quality assurance. However, it's concerning that Samsung's software update process is still not as efficient as that of other companies.

## Samsung Develops DDR5-7200 512GB RAM

Samsung has announced the development of the industry's first DDR5-7200 512GB memory module. This new memory technology offers 40% higher performance and twice the capacity of DDR4 memory at a mere 1.1V. The company's DDR5-7200 memory module is built with eight-stacked DDR5 dies that are interconnected with TSV (through-silicon-via) technology. This design improvement allows for a height reduction on the stacks, making it more efficient.

Samsung's DDR5-7200 memory module operates at 1.1V, which is significantly lower than the voltage required for DDR4. This improved power efficiency is made possible by the high-efficiency power management IC (PMIC), voltage regulator, and the High-K Metal gate process. The company's PMIC not only contributes to lower voltage operation but also reduces noise in the process.

## Samsung Expands Solid-State Battery Technology

Samsung has announced that it will mass-produce solid-state batteries for 'super premium' EVs. These batteries promise to improve vehicle range, decrease charging times, and eliminate the risk of battery fires. The company's solid-state battery technology will initially go into "super premium EVs" and will offer 900 to 1,000 kilometers (559-621 miles) of range and improved safety.

Samsung's solid-state battery technology is being developed in partnership with various automakers, including Hyundai, Stellantis, and General Motors. The company plans to start mass-producing these batteries as soon as 2027, with LG Energy also planning to enter the market by 2030. Samsung's solid-state battery technology has the potential to revolutionize the EV industry, offering improved performance and safety.

## Samsung to Block Apps That Spam Notifications with Ads

Samsung has announced a new feature for Galaxy phones that allows users to easily block apps that spam notifications with ads. This feature is a welcome addition to the company's software, as it helps users avoid annoying ads and protects their personal data.

The new feature is part of Samsung's efforts to improve the overall user experience on its Galaxy phones. The company has been working to provide more control to users over their notification settings, and this new feature is a step in the right direction. However, it remains to be seen how effective this feature will be in blocking ads and how users will respond to it.
