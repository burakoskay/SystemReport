## Stricter Oversight for US Grants

The US government has proposed new funding rules that would give it more control over grant funding, allowing for the cancellation of grants at any time and making peer review optional. The rules would also allow political staff to screen grants for forbidden topics. This move is seen as a significant shift in the grant-making process, giving the government more flexibility to cancel grants if it deems them not in line with its priorities or if it finds that the research is not making progress.

The proposed rules have raised concerns among researchers and scientists who rely on grant funding for their work. They argue that the new rules would give the government too much control over the research process and could lead to politicization of funding decisions. The rules could also lead to a decrease in the quality of research, as researchers may feel pressured to tailor their research to fit the government's priorities rather than pursuing their own research questions.
