# OpenAI Breaks Microsoft's Hold

OpenAI has renegotiated its deal with Microsoft, ending the exclusivity that had tied its products to Azure. This new agreement lets OpenAI sell its products on Amazon Web Services (AWS) and other cloud providers, while Microsoft maintains a significant revenue-sharing arrangement.

## A Shift in Cloud Strategy

The original partnership between OpenAI and Microsoft, announced in 2019, made Azure the exclusive cloud provider for OpenAI's products. This changed in January 2025 when Microsoft announced an updated agreement, stating that OpenAI products would ship first on Azure, unless Microsoft couldn't support the necessary capabilities. Now, OpenAI can serve all its products across any cloud provider.

Microsoft will remain OpenAI's primary cloud partner, but OpenAI has gained flexibility in where it can host its products. This development comes as OpenAI looks to expand its reach, particularly in the enterprise sector. By allowing its models to run on multiple cloud platforms, OpenAI can more easily cater to businesses with diverse infrastructure needs.

The shift in cloud strategy is significant, as it allows OpenAI to tap into the growing demand for cloud-based AI solutions. According to a report by MarketsandMarkets, the cloud AI market is expected to grow from $4.2 billion in 2020 to $21.1 billion by 2025, at a Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 38.1% during the forecast period.

## The Road to This Point

The partnership between Microsoft and OpenAI began in 2016, with a focus on building AI supercomputing capabilities. Microsoft has invested heavily in OpenAI, committing billions of dollars to support its research and development. The two companies have collaborated on several high-profile AI projects, including GitHub Copilot, DALL·E 2, and ChatGPT.

However, the exclusivity clause in their agreement had become a point of contention. By dropping this clause, OpenAI gains more control over its product deployment strategy. This move also reflects OpenAI's growing maturity as a company, as it seeks to balance its relationship with Microsoft while exploring opportunities with other cloud providers.

## Technical Mechanics

The amended agreement clears the way for OpenAI models to run on Amazon Bedrock, a fully managed service that makes it easy for developers to build and deploy large language models. This integration could make OpenAI's AI capabilities more accessible to a broader range of developers and businesses.

OpenAI's models are built using a range of technologies, including the company's proprietary GPT architecture. The models are trained on large datasets and can be fine-tuned for specific applications. By making these models available on Amazon Bedrock, OpenAI can tap into the growing demand for cloud-based AI solutions.
