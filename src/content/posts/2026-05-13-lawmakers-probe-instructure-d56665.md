## Data Breaches Under Scrutiny
Lawmakers are demanding answers from Instructure after two data breaches exposed reams of student data from the company's Canvas software. The breaches, which occurred on the flagship student data platform, have raised concerns about the security of sensitive information.

The US House lawmakers are seeking to understand how hackers were able to break into Instructure's systems not once, but twice, and steal large amounts of data from students who use the Canvas software. The lawmakers' inquiry is focused on the scope and blast radius of the breaches, as well as the measures Instructure is taking to prevent similar incidents in the future. According to reports, the breaches resulted in the theft of sensitive information, including student records and personal data.

## Employee Protests at Meta
Meanwhile, Meta employees are protesting the company's decision to use employees' mouse movements and keystrokes to train AI models. The program, which was announced last month, has been met with resistance from employees who are concerned about the potential invasion of their privacy. The protest highlights the tension between the need for companies to collect data to improve their services and the need to protect employees' personal information.
