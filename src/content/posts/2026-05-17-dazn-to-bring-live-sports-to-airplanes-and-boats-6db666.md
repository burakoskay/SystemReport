## Live Sports on the Go

DAZN, a global sports streaming outlet backed by billionaire Len Blavatnik, is planning to launch live streaming access for viewers traveling on airplanes and boats. The expansion, planned for 2027, is being called DAZN Inflight. It would address a significant pain point for sports fans who travel during live events. Currently, in-flight entertainment options are limited, and live sports streaming services are scarce.

The move is a strategic step for DAZN, which has core offerings including boxing and soccer, as well as a mix of NFL, Major League Baseball, and NBA in select markets. To deliver live sports to airplanes and boats, DAZN will rely on advancements in technology and high-speed internet access. The company claims its built-for-sport global technology platform, combined with its deep relationships with rights holders, competitions, and partners, positions it to bring live sports to previously inaccessible audiences.

## Securing Rights and Technical Challenges

One of the significant challenges DAZN faces is securing rights to broadcast live sports. The company has different rights to various sports across the world. Securing these rights can be complex and costly. For instance, major sports leagues like the NFL, NBA, and MLB have strict requirements for broadcasting their games, including restrictions on streaming and territorial limitations.

To overcome these challenges, DAZN will need to negotiate with rights holders and develop technology that can efficiently stream live sports to moving vehicles. The company will likely partner with airlines, cruise ship operators, and technology providers to ensure a seamless viewing experience. This may involve developing customized apps or integrating with existing in-flight entertainment systems.
