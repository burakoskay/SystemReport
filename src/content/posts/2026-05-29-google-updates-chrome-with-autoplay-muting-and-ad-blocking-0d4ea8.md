## Google Chrome Tackles Annoying Web Experiences

Google has released a new version of Chrome, which can protect users from some of the most frustrating experiences on the web. The browser now allows users to permanently mute websites that autoplay videos, and will soon gain a built-in ad blocker to prevent intrusive ads.

The update, Chrome 64, is available on Windows, Mac, and Linux. To mute a site that automatically plays videos, users need to click the View Site Information symbol on the left-hand edge of the omnibar. Once the website is muted, it will not automatically play videos with sound again until the user unmutes it.

Autoplay muting is a response to user complaints about unexpected audio. According to Google, this feature is one of the top user requests. The company has also been working to reduce the number of annoying ads on the web.

## Confronting Cleverly Disguised Links and Popups

Chrome 64 also confronts the issue of cleverly disguised links and popups. The browser will prevent sites from tricking users into opening new windows or tabs by making links to third-party websites look like something else. These links are often disguised as play buttons or close symbols, and can take users to unwanted websites.

According to Google, 20% of feedback reports from Chrome users on desktop include complaints about encountering unwanted content. The new update aims to address this issue by providing users with more control over their browsing experience.
