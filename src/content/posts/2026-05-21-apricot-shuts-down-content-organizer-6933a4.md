## Apricot's Demise

Apricot's shutdown was announced on Hacker News in December 2025. The team expressed gratitude to users who had trusted Apricot to manage their content. The announcement came as a surprise to many who had come to rely on the service to organize their digital lives.

## How Apricot Worked

Apricot's web app allowed users to follow their favorite TV shows, podcasts, YouTube channels, subreddits, newsletters, and blogs in one place. The service aimed to help users see the good stuff and cut the clutter. Users could scroll through items by feed, platform, or a combined, personalized feed. Apricot's AI-powered technology sorted and summarized content to help users decide what to spend time on, cutting through click-bait and prioritizing high-quality content.

## The Problem with Content Overload

The shutdown raises questions about managing online content. Users struggle to find relevant and high-quality content in an increasingly crowded digital landscape. Apricot's approach focused on curation, helping users to prioritize and make sense of the vast amounts of content available to them. The service's goal was to simplify content management by consolidating multiple platforms in one place, eliminating the need to switch from app to app.
