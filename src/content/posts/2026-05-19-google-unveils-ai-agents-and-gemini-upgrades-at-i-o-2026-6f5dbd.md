## Google Introduces AI Agents
Google launched AI-powered information agents that monitor topics in the background and proactively alert users to updates and changes. According to Google, these agents can track any subject and send notifications. This move is a direct response to the growing need for personalized information management. The AI agents use natural language processing to monitor topics and provide relevant updates. 
## Gemini Upgrades
Google's Gemini line received two upgrades at I/O. Gemini 3.5 Flash is a more efficient version that enables practical agentic AI for everyday tasks. Gemini Omni can generate anything from any input, starting with video. The Gemini 3.5 Flash is expected to significantly improve the performance of AI-powered applications, making them more accessible to a wider range of users. 
## Audio Glasses
Google announced audio glasses, a wearable that relies on verbal commands to interact with the Google ecosystem, including Gemini. The device lets users issue commands and receive responses without looking at a screen. This new product is part of Google's efforts to expand its presence in the wearables market and provide a more seamless user experience. 
## AI Studio and Accessibility
Google's AI Studio is a web-based tool that can generate native Android apps in minutes. Google also emphasized its AI tools are designed for accessibility to teachers and small businesses. The Gemini for Science collection includes three new research-workflow features. The AI Studio is expected to democratize app development, allowing non-technical users to create their own apps.
