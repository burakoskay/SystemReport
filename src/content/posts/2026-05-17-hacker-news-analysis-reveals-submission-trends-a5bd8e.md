## Hacker News Submissions: A Statistical Analysis

Hacker News, a popular link aggregator for the technology and startup community, has been analyzed in a statistical study to understand its impact on the tech industry. The study, which downloaded 1,265,114 Hacker News submissions from the official API, gathered interesting statistics on the platform's growth and user engagement.

The analysis shows that Hacker News had a growth rate similar to that of startups funded by Y Combinator, with monthly submissions nearly tripling from 12k in 2010 to 31k in 2011. However, the number of submissions has been trending downward since 2011, which may be attributed to the implementation of a proprietary anti-spam algorithm that "kills" submissions.

## Understanding Hacker News Engagement

On Hacker News, users can upvote submissions, which determines their position on the front page of the site. A simple heuristic for calculating exposure from a Hacker News front page submission is 100 page views per point minimum. The analysis reveals that the vast majority of submissions don't even hit 10 points, with an average of 9.51 points per submission.

## Top-Scoring Submissions

The growth rate of top-scoring submissions is correlated with the growth rate of Hacker News submissions themselves. The number of points a post receives is also dependent on the number of users; as Hacker News grows, the number of users grows as well. Even though the front page cycles frequently, there is still room for great content.

## Best Time to Post

The analysis also explores the best time to post on Hacker News to maximize visibility. By observing the average number of submissions for each combination of submission hour and weekday, the study finds that Hacker News activity is most active at around 12 PM EST / 9 AM PST, with about 40 submissions per hour.

## Effects of a Hacker News Submission

A case study on the effects of a Hacker News submission reveals that a single post can generate significant traffic and engagement. A blog post submitted to Hacker News received over 10,000 visitors and 43 comments, with 65% of traffic originating from Hacker News-owned URLs.

## History of Hacker News

Hacker News was launched in 2007 by Paul Graham, a well-known entrepreneur and investor. The platform was initially created as a way for the Y Combinator community to share and discuss news and ideas related to technology and startups. Over the years, Hacker News has evolved to become a major platform for tech enthusiasts and entrepreneurs, with a strong focus on community engagement and user-generated content.

## Technical Mechanics

The technical mechanics behind Hacker News are designed to promote high-quality content and engage users. The platform uses a voting system to surface the most popular and relevant submissions, with users able to upvote or downvote posts to express their opinions. The algorithm used to determine the front page of Hacker News is designed to prioritize submissions with high engagement, such as comments and upvotes.
