## Anthropic's $15 Billion Bet on SpaceX's AI Compute

Anthropic has agreed to pay SpaceX $1.25 billion per month, or $15 billion annually, for the next three years to rent compute capacity on SpaceX's Colossus and Colossus II data centers. This massive deal, disclosed in SpaceX's recent S-1 filing, underscores the intense demand for AI infrastructure and the lengths companies are willing to go to secure it.

The agreement, signed in May 2026, gives Anthropic access to more than 300 megawatts of new capacity, including over 220,000 NVIDIA GPUs. This will directly improve capacity for Anthropic's Claude Pro and Claude Max subscribers. Anthropic is also exploring opportunities to develop multiple gigawatts of orbital AI compute capacity with SpaceX.

## The Stakes of the Deal

The deal is a significant boost to SpaceX's AI infrastructure business, which is rapidly becoming a major contributor to the company's revenue. SpaceX's S-1 filing reveals that the AI segment, which includes the AI infrastructure inherited from xAI, is burning cash to chase capacity. The filing also highlights the importance of the AI business to SpaceX's future growth.

Anthropic's investment in AI infrastructure comes as the company is rapidly expanding its offerings. The company recently increased usage limits for Claude Code and the Claude API, and is opening a new office in Milan. The deal with SpaceX is part of Anthropic's broader strategy to build out its AI capabilities and meet growing demand from enterprise customers.
