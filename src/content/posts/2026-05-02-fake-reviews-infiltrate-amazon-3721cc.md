## The Problem of Fake or Incentivised Reviews
Fake or incentivised reviews are a significant issue on Amazon, with hundreds of tech products from unknown brands receiving top reviews from unverified purchasers. A 2019 investigation by Which? found that many of these reviews showed telltale signs of being fake or incentivised.

The investigation, conducted on April 16, 2019, revealed that popular accessories such as smartwatches and headphones were the worst offenders, with hundreds of unverified reviews being given to products listed on the same day or within a short space of time.

## The Impact on Consumers
The presence of fake or incentivised reviews on Amazon can have serious consequences for consumers. It can lead to people making purchasing decisions based on false information. This can result in financial losses for consumers and damage to Amazon's reputation.

## The Challenge of Identifying Fake Reviews
Identifying fake reviews can be a challenging task, especially given the large number of reviews on Amazon. According to an analysis of ~10k professional product reviews, certain keywords and phrases were more commonly used in fake reviews. The analysis, conducted by 371 experts and updated on March 25, 2026, found that expert analysis can help identify patterns and trends that may indicate the presence of fake reviews.

## The Broader Industry Context
The issue of fake reviews is not unique to Amazon. Other e-commerce platforms also struggle with this problem. However, Amazon's large market share and dominance in the online retail space make it a prime target for fake reviewers. The company must address this issue to maintain trust in its review system and protect its customers.

## The History of Fake Reviews
Fake reviews have been a problem on Amazon for several years. In 2019, Which? conducted an investigation that found hundreds of tech products from unknown brands had fake or incentivised reviews. Since then, Amazon has taken steps to address the issue, including implementing machine learning algorithms to detect and remove fake reviews. However, the problem persists, and Amazon must continue to evolve its strategies to stay ahead of fake reviewers.

## The Technical Mechanics
The process of creating and detecting fake reviews involves complex algorithms and machine learning models. Amazon uses natural language processing and machine learning to analyze reviews and detect patterns that may indicate fake reviews. However, fake reviewers can also use sophisticated techniques to evade detection, including using multiple accounts and manipulating review content.

## Downstream Implications
The presence of fake reviews on Amazon can have significant downstream implications for consumers, businesses, and the company itself. For consumers, fake reviews can lead to poor purchasing decisions and financial losses. For businesses, fake reviews can damage their reputation and lead to lost sales. For Amazon, fake reviews can erode trust in its review system and damage its brand reputation.

## The Impact on Amazon's Business Model
Amazon's business model relies heavily on its review system. The company uses reviews to determine the visibility of products on its platform, with products having more reviews and higher ratings appearing higher in search results. However, if the review system is compromised by fake reviews, it can lead to a loss of trust among consumers and a decline in sales for legitimate businesses.

## The Need for Continuous Improvement
To combat the issue of fake reviews, Amazon must continuously improve its detection and removal algorithms. The company must also work with consumers and businesses to identify and report fake reviews. Additionally, Amazon can implement measures such as requiring reviewers to have a verified purchase history or limiting the number of reviews that can be left by a single user.

## Conclusion
The issue of fake or incentivised reviews on Amazon is a serious one, and it has significant implications for consumers and the company itself. Amazon must address this problem to maintain trust in its review system and protect its customers. The company must continue to evolve its strategies to stay ahead of fake reviewers and ensure that its review system remains a trusted source of information for consumers.
