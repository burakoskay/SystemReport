A Google engineer made $1.2 million on insider bets tied to the company’s 2025 Year in Search campaign, using wagers placed on the Polymarket platform. The engineer risked over $2.7 million on these bets, leading to a federal indictment for misuse of non-public information. This case comes as Illinois lawmakers passed a bill requiring companies like OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google to submit their safety practices to independent reviewers. Governor JB Pritzker has endorsed the measure. 
## Insider Trading Meets AI Ethics 
Federal prosecutors argue that the engineer’s conduct violates securities law. The case highlights a gap in how tech firms police internal data. This lack of oversight can have significant consequences, particularly in the development of AI systems where data integrity is crucial. The need for robust safety protocols in AI development is further emphasized by the fact that AI systems can have far-reaching impacts on society, from influencing public opinion to making life-or-death decisions. 
## Illinois’ Hardest AI Safety Bill 
The new Illinois legislation requires third-party verification of safety standards for AI systems. Independent auditors must certify that the models meet criteria around bias, disinformation, and harmful output. This move reflects a growing concern about the potential risks associated with AI and the need for rigorous safety protocols. The bill's focus on third-party verification is significant, as it ensures that AI systems are evaluated by independent experts rather than the companies that develop them. 
## Industry Context 
The AI safety bill passed in Illinois is part of a broader trend towards increased regulation of AI and technology companies. As these companies continue to develop and deploy AI systems, there is a growing recognition of the need for robust safety standards and oversight mechanisms. Companies like Google, OpenAI, and Anthropic will need to adapt to these new requirements, which could impact their development timelines and business models. The bill's passage also reflects a shift in the way that policymakers think about AI, from viewing it as a largely unregulated field to recognizing the need for careful oversight and regulation.

The development of AI systems involves complex technical processes, including data collection, model training, and testing.
