## Introduction to Petridish
Petridish is a crowdfunding platform for science that plans to transform the way research is funded. On Petridish, science-lovers can connect directly with researchers to help them launch new projects. For example, one of the scientists on the site is offering naming rights to new species of animals. Disciplines of projects include those centered around biology, ecology, geology, astronomy, and more. This direct connection between researchers and the public could significantly alter the landscape of scientific research funding.

## How Petridish Works
Researchers can post materials about themselves and their research on the site, and the public can discover projects that are exciting to them. In exchange for contributing to the project, backers receive insider updates on the research, naming rights to new discoveries, and other souvenirs from the work. Projects will only be funded if they reach their goal before the deadline set by the researcher. Petridish will take 5 percent of the donation for each transaction. This model incentivizes researchers to create engaging project proposals and allows backers to feel invested in the research process.

## SpaceX Recovers Dragon Capsule
SpaceX recovered the Dragon capsule loaded with science and research from the International Space Station. The capsule was carrying 4,500 pounds of supplies and scientific experiment equipment when it made its way to the ISS. Dragon made its way back to earth, departing the space station and beginning its re-entry into Earth’s atmosphere. A five-hour trip back into Earth’s sky included a deorbiting burn, and then the capsule splashed down in the Pacific Ocean. The successful recovery of the Dragon capsule marks a significant milestone in SpaceX's ongoing efforts to support scientific research in space.

## History of Crowdfunding in Science
The concept of crowdfunding for scientific research is not new, but platforms like Petridish are making it more accessible and mainstream. Previously, scientists often relied on government grants or private donations to fund their research. However, with the rise of crowdfunding platforms, researchers can now reach a wider audience and secure funding for projects that might not have been possible through traditional means. For instance, platforms like Kickstarter and Indiegogo have been used to fund various scientific projects, from documentary films to technological innovations.
