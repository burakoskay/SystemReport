## Harvard Analyzes 94,000 Nights of Apple Watch Sleep Data to Study Menopause

Researchers at Harvard published findings analyzing over 94,000 nights of Apple Watch sleep data to understand sleep pattern changes during perimenopause. The study, detailed by 9to5Mac, uses consumer wearables to track physiological shifts in real-world populations. Apple Watch's continuous heart rate and sleep tracking provided granular data across multiple cycles.

The study identifies distinct sleep disruption patterns correlated with hormonal fluctuations in perimenopausal women. Researchers found irregular sleep latency and reduced deep sleep duration compared to premenopausal baselines. These findings could inform targeted interventions for sleep disorders in midlife women, leveraging wearable health data.

## Apple Confirms No Armed Group Ties in 2023 Conflict Minerals Audit

Apple filed its annual conflict minerals report with the SEC, confirming suppliers showed no evidence linking them to armed groups in conflict zones. The disclosure covers materials used in iPhone, Mac, and other product manufacturing for fiscal year 2023. The company's sourcing policy excludes minerals from the Democratic Republic of Congo and adjoining countries.

The report follows standard supply chain due diligence procedures Apple has maintained since 2013. While the findings show compliance, critics argue the reporting lacks transparency about subcontractors in secondary supply chains. 9to5Mac notes Apple remains under pressure from human rights groups to publish more detailed supplier locations.

## Union Protests Towson Apple Store Closure, First US Unionized Retail Location

The IAM Union staged a protest outside Apple's Towson, Maryland store after the company announced its closure. This marks the first unionized Apple retail location in the United States. Union representatives highlighted concerns about store closures threatening retail worker protections negotiated in 2023.

The Towson closure follows Apple's restructuring of its regional retail strategy. Union leaders argue the decision undermines the collective bargaining agreement reached with Retail Workers United. Protesters demonstrated outside the store as employees processed final transactions on April 20, 2024.

## Age of Empires II Finally Lands on macOS After 20-Year Absence

Age of Empires II: Definitive Edition became available on macOS platforms in April 2024, fulfilling a long-awaited request from Mac gamers. The port includes updated graphics, gameplay enhancements, and cross-platform multiplayer support. 9to5Mac notes this follows similar ports of Age of Empires IV to macOS in 2023.

The release addresses a key gap in Apple's gaming ecosystem. While macOS gaming market share remains below 5%, ports like this continue to expand Steam Play's reach. However, performance benchmarks show 20% lower frame rates compared to Windows 11 on identical hardware configurations.

## What to Watch as Wearables Meet Medical Research

The Harvard study highlights wearable data's potential for large-scale health research. Apple Watch's sleep tracking accuracy will face regulatory scrutiny as studies like this seek medical validation. The FDA's upcoming draft guidelines on consumer health device data could shape future research partnerships.

Apple's conflict minerals reporting will intersect with new EU regulations requiring supply chain transparency. The Digital Product Passport requirement for electronics, set to take effect in 2025, may force Apple to disclose more granular supplier data than its current SEC filings.

Retail workers across the US will monitor how the Towson closure affects unionization efforts. The National Labor Relations Board is reviewing Apple's store closure policies under the National Labor Relations Act. Retail Workers United plans to file charges if they determine anti-union bias influenced the Towson decision.

Finally, macOS gamers should track Microsoft's potential acquisition of Activision Blizzard. The transaction could accelerate or stall ports of classic PC games to Apple's platform, depending on regulatory outcomes in ongoing antitrust litigation.
