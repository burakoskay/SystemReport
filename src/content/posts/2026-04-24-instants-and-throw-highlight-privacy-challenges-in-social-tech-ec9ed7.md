## Instagram tests Instants for ephemeral sharing

Instagram is testing Instants, an app that lets users share photos that disappear after a single view. The app allows users to share disappearing photos with friends that can be viewed only once and remain available for 24 hours. This move by Instagram can be seen as a direct response to the growing demand for ephemeral content, a trend popularized by Snapchat. By limiting the visibility of photos to a single view, Instagram aims to provide users with a more private and secure way of sharing content. The feature's success will depend on its ability to balance user engagement with the need for safety and moderation.

## The rise of anonymous Q&A platforms

Throw is a community-driven Q&A platform that allows users to post queries anonymously and receive answers from verified experts. The platform positions itself as a space for users to share concerns and wisdom while feeling supported, particularly for those dealing with loneliness and mental health struggles. Throw's approach to anonymous interactions has sparked debate about the benefits and drawbacks of such platforms. On one hand, they provide a safe space for users to open up about sensitive topics; on the other hand, they raise concerns about the potential for misuse and the spread of misinformation. The platform's focus on empathy and expertise could help mitigate these risks.

## AI misuse leads to prison sentence

A man was sentenced to five years in prison for using AI to fabricate a sighting of a runaway wolf. The wolf had burrowed out of its zoo enclosure, and the AI-generated claim added sensational details. This incident highlights the risks associated with the misuse of AI-generated content. As AI technology becomes more sophisticated, the potential for malicious actors to exploit it for their own gain increases. The case raises important questions about the need for regulation and accountability in the development and deployment of AI systems. It also underscores the importance of verifying information, especially when it comes to sensitive or potentially harmful content.
