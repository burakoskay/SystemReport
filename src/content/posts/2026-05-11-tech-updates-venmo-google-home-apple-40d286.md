## Venmo Tightens Payment Defaults
Venmo's redesigned app sets post visibility to 'friends only' by default for new users. This change offers more discreet payments, reflecting a growing emphasis on user privacy in financial services. By doing so, Venmo is taking a step towards providing users with more control over their payment data, which is increasingly becoming a key differentiator in the financial services industry. The move is likely to be welcomed by users who value privacy and may help Venmo to differentiate itself from competitors.

The decision to change the default settings to 'friends only' is a significant one, as it reflects a shift towards greater user control and privacy. This change is also in line with broader industry trends, which are seeing a greater emphasis on user data protection and security. ## Google Home Speeds Up Gemini
Google Home's new update focuses on performance improvements for Gemini and enhances voice command functionality. The 'Ask Home' feature now supports voice commands, making interactions more seamless. Google aims to make its Home products more intuitive by integrating advanced AI capabilities. This move is part of Google's broader strategy to improve the user experience of its smart home devices, which is critical for the company's ambitions in the rapidly growing smart home market.

The update to Google Home is significant, as it reflects the company's efforts to improve the performance and functionality of its smart home devices. The integration of advanced AI capabilities is likely to make Google Home more competitive in the smart home market, which is seeing rapid growth. The company's focus on user experience is also likely to be welcomed by users, who are increasingly expecting seamless and intuitive interactions with their smart home devices.
