## Department of Labor's DEI Directive

The US Department of Labor has instructed employees to report colleagues prioritizing diversity, equity, and inclusion efforts. The email, obtained by WIRED, tells workers to report behavior that predates Donald Trump's second inauguration. This directive has raised concerns among employees, with one worker describing it as a "reminder to narc on your coworkers."

The email's impact on the workplace remains to be seen, but it is clear that the Department of Labor is taking a hardline stance on DEI efforts. This move is likely to be met with resistance from employees and advocacy groups who see DEI initiatives as essential to creating a more inclusive workplace.

The Department of Labor's directive is part of a broader effort to roll back DEI initiatives across the federal government. This effort has been driven by conservative lawmakers and activists who argue that DEI initiatives are unnecessary and divisive.

## Prisoners on Strike

Thousands of prisoners are participating in a 19-day strike to protest ultra-low pay, forced labor, and poor conditions in prisons across the US. The strike, organized by incarcerated prisoner-rights advocates, is planned to conclude on the 47th anniversary of the 1971 Attica prison riot. Inmates plan to refuse to work, and a smaller number plan to go on hunger strikes and conduct sit-ins.

The strike highlights the harsh conditions faced by prisoners in the US. Many prisoners are forced to work for minimal pay, often in hazardous conditions, while also being denied basic rights and dignity. The strike is a call to action for policymakers to address these issues and treat prisoners with the respect and dignity they deserve.

The prison strike is also a reflection of the broader issues facing the US prison system. The US has one of the highest incarceration rates in the world, and prisons are often plagued by overcrowding, violence, and poor conditions.

## The Amazon Union Drive
