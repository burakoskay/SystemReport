# Introduction
Google unveiled audio-powered smart glasses and a revamped Gemini app at I/O 2026, signaling a push to make its AI services the default interface for everyday tasks. The company introduced the devices under the name "audio glasses," promised voice-only interaction, and paired them with Gemini, its flagship large-language model. At the same event, Google also announced Universal Cart, a cross-site shopping tracker, and an update that recasts the Gemini app as an all-purpose AI hub competing with ChatGPT and Claude. Google says it's designed the app to be accessible to everyone, from teachers to small business owners.

## Audio Glasses
Google is calling the new devices "audio glasses," in that users will be able to issue verbal commands to them and get things done via its ecosystem of apps and services, including Gemini. Google takes a page out of Meta’s book with this approach. By integrating voice commands into everyday tasks, Google aims to make its AI services more intuitive and user-friendly. The glasses are designed to be used hands-free, allowing users to interact with their surroundings while still accessing information and services. This approach could potentially revolutionize the way people interact with technology, making it more seamless and integrated into daily life.

## Gemini App
The Gemini app received an update that expands it beyond a chatbot. The update adds integrations with Google Workspace, Maps, and the new audio glasses, turning the app into a single point of entry for a range of AI-driven tasks. This move signals Google’s push to turn its Gemini app into an all-purpose AI hub rather than a stand-alone chatbot. The app's new features include the ability to summarize long documents, translate languages, and provide personalized recommendations. By integrating these features into a single app, Google aims to make its AI services more accessible and user-friendly.

## Universal Cart
Most people shop across multiple devices, many retailers, and over the course of many days, which is why Google is launching Universal Cart. This feature aims to simplify the shopping experience by allowing users to track their purchases across different sites and devices. Universal Cart will also allow users to compare prices, read reviews, and make purchases directly from the app. By streamlining the shopping experience, Google hopes to make its AI services more indispensable to users.

## Android XR
Android XR is finally starting to feel real. Google has a lot to prove, but its smart glasses will have a few big advantages. The company’s focus on audio-powered interaction and AI-driven tasks may give it an edge in the emerging XR market. Android XR is an operating system designed specifically for extended reality (XR) devices, such as smart glasses and head-mounted displays. By developing its own XR operating system, Google aims to create a more seamless and integrated experience for users.
