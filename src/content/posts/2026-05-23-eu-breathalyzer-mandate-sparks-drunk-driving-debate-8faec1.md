# EU Breathalyzer Mandate Sparks Drunk-Driving Debate

## EU Breathalyzer Mandate Sparks Drunk-Driving Debate

All vehicles sold in the EU must now be equipped with a breathalyzer system, part of a broader strategy to eliminate drunk-driving-related deaths and injuries by 2050. The measure is a significant step towards reducing road fatalities.

## Industry Context

The EU's drunk-driving elimination strategy is part of a larger effort to reduce road fatalities. According to the European Commission, there were over 22,000 road fatalities in the EU in 2020. The commission aims to reduce this number to zero by 2050. The breathalyzer mandate is a key component of this strategy. Several countries have already implemented similar measures, with varying degrees of success. For example, Sweden has seen a significant reduction in drunk-driving incidents since introducing a similar breathalyzer system.

## Technical Mechanics

The breathalyzer system uses a combination of sensors and software to detect the driver's blood alcohol concentration. The system typically includes a breathalyzer device, a vehicle's onboard computer, and a display screen. When the driver blows into the breathalyzer, the device analyzes the sample and sends the results to the vehicle's onboard computer. If the driver's blood alcohol concentration is above the predetermined limit, the vehicle will not start.
