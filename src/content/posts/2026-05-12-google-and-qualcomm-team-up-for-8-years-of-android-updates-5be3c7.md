## Google and Qualcomm's Big Push for Android Updates

In a significant move to address Android's long-standing issue with software updates, Google and Qualcomm have joined forces to provide extended support for Android devices. This partnership aims to offer up to 8 years of software updates for devices powered by Qualcomm's latest chipsets.

## How It Works

The extended support window will apply to Android devices with the latest Qualcomm chipsets, starting with the Snapdragon 8 Elite mobile platform. This means that devices running on this platform, such as the OnePlus 13 and Galaxy S25, will receive up to 8 years of security patches and OS updates. The same policy will be applied to the company's new Snapdragon 8 and Snapdragon 7-series chips later this year.

## The Impact on Android Devices

This move could make your next phone more useful for longer. With Qualcomm's help, OEMs will be able to provide 8 years of security patches, and there will also be at least two updates to the vendor's Android Common Kernel during that time. This will make it easier for OEMs to release full Android OS updates even toward the end of a device's lifespan.

## A Long-Term Solution to Android's Update Problem

Google's open approach to Android initially led to a fragmented ecosystem where devices struggled to receive timely updates. Successive projects like Project Treble and Project Mainline have aimed to improve Android software support. The combination of consistent vendor layer support and fresh kernels will, according to Qualcomm, make it faster and easier for OEMs to deploy updates.

## Technical Mechanics: How This Partnership Enables Extended Support

The partnership between Google and Qualcomm is built on the foundation of Google's Project Treble, which was introduced over three years ago. Project Treble aimed to speed up software updates by rearchitecting Android's software stack. However, this architecture increased the complexity and engineering costs associated with providing Android OS update support for chipsets. 

Qualcomm and Google have worked together to address these challenges. By providing extended vendor support to OEMs building on Qualcomm's most powerful chips, they are enabling a more streamlined update process. This involves collaboration on the Android Common Kernel and consistent support for vendor layers, which are critical components in ensuring that devices can receive timely and secure updates.
