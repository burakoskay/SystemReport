The Federal Communications Commission (FCC) is seeking public comment on whether ABC's daytime talk show The View's content decisions are 'based on newsworthiness,' a criterion typically applied to news shows.

## What's at Stake
The classification of The View could affect its treatment under broadcasting regulations, particularly regarding its approach to news and current events. If The View is deemed a news show, it may be subject to stricter regulations and guidelines, potentially impacting its editorial freedom. Conversely, if it is not considered a news show, it may be exempt from certain regulations, but could also face criticism for not adhering to journalistic standards.

The View covers news and features interviews with newsmakers, but its format and approach differ significantly from traditional news programs. The show is known for its opinion-driven discussions and debates, often focusing on current events, politics, and social issues. While it does cover news, its primary focus is on providing a platform for discussion and analysis rather than straightforward reporting.
