Apple is stacking new talent, software tweaks and hardware leadership changes to keep its ecosystem humming. The moves signal a push to tighten user experience while accelerating the pipeline for next‑gen devices.

Rhea See​horn signed on for a second Apple Original film called *Running*. iOS 26 shipped a long‑awaited order‑tracking fix for Wallet. Apple TV announced four new Peanuts premieres for the summer. And Johny Srouji, newly named chief hardware officer, is overhauling processes to speed up future products after John Ternus was slated to become CEO on Sept. 1.

## Apple’s Talent Push with *Running*

The casting of Rhea See​horn marks Apple’s second foray into original film with the actress known for her breakout role on *Better Call Saul*. The project, titled *Running*, adds a recognizable face to Apple Original Films’ roster. Apple has not disclosed a release date or plot details, but the announcement alone shows the company’s confidence in star power to draw subscribers.

For a platform that already houses high‑budget series, a film starring See​horn suggests Apple is betting on narrative depth rather than just episodic content. The move could attract viewers who follow her work, expanding the audience beyond tech‑savvy early adopters. It also hints that Apple will continue to fund original movies as a core part of its streaming strategy.

## iOS 26 Wallet Order‑Tracking Fix

iOS 26 finally delivered an order‑tracking feature inside the Wallet app that many users have requested for years. The update lets shoppers see shipment status directly from the stored receipt, eliminating the need to open separate retailer apps. Apple’s rollout instructions show a new “Track Order” button on supported cards.

The fix arrives after a long period of user complaints about the missing functionality. By integrating logistics into Wallet, Apple tightens the loop between purchase and delivery, reinforcing the app’s role as a one‑stop shop for payments. Developers will need to adopt the new API to surface tracking data, but the change is already live for compatible merchants.

## Summer Peanuts Premieres on Apple TV

Apple TV confirmed four new Peanuts specials will debut this summer. The streamer remains the exclusive home for the Charles Schulz franchise, a hold that has helped it stand out in a crowded streaming market. The announcement did not list titles or exact dates, only that the premieres will roll out over the summer months.

The timing aligns with the platform’s push to add family‑friendly content ahead of the back‑to‑school season. By refreshing the Peanuts library, Apple TV hopes to retain existing subscribers and lure new households looking for nostalgic, safe‑to‑watch programming. The move also underscores Apple’s strategy of leveraging legacy IP to complement its original productions.

## Srouji’s Hardware Acceleration Plan

Bloomberg reported that Johny Srouji, now Apple’s chief hardware officer, is reshaping internal workflows to speed up product development. The change follows the board’s decision to install John Ternus as CEO effective Sept. 1. Srouji’s expanded remit gives him authority over silicon, packaging and industrial design teams.

According to the report, Srouji is cutting redundancies and tightening review cycles. He is also pushing for tighter integration between hardware and software groups to reduce time‑to‑market for future devices. The plan is a direct response to competitive pressure from rivals who have shortened their launch cadences.

## What to Watch

The next quarter will reveal whether Apple’s content bets and hardware reforms translate into measurable gains. Track the release window for *Running* and the performance of the Peanuts specials in subscriber metrics. Monitor iOS 26 adoption rates to see how quickly users engage with the new Wallet tracking. Finally, watch for any public statements from Srouji or Ternus on the revised hardware roadmap, especially any hints about upcoming silicon generations.
