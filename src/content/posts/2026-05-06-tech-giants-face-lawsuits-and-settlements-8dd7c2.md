## Apple Settles Lawsuit Over Siri's AI Features

Apple has agreed to pay $250 million to settle a class-action lawsuit over allegedly overpromising the arrival of Siri's AI features. The lawsuit, filed on behalf of iPhone users, claimed that Apple's marketing misled customers about the capabilities of its virtual assistant. This move comes as part of a broader trend of tech companies facing scrutiny over their marketing practices.

The settlement will provide eligible iPhone users with a payout of up to $95 per device. Users who bought an iPhone 15 or 16 in the US could be eligible for the payment. To put this into perspective, Apple's iPhone sales have been a significant contributor to the company's revenue, with the iPhone 15 and 16 being among the most popular models.

## Google and Epic Games Reach Settlement Over Android App Store Fees

Google has agreed to settle a lawsuit with Epic Games, the maker of Fortnite, over Android app store fees. The settlement will allow alternative app stores to register with Google and become first-class citizens on the Android platform. This development could have far-reaching implications for the app store ecosystem, potentially leading to increased competition and innovation.

The settlement is a significant victory for Epic Games, which had been challenging Google's app store fees and business practices. The terms of the settlement will require Google to make changes to its app store policies, allowing for more flexibility and competition.

## Uber Settles Lawsuits with Drivers, Maintains Contractor Status

Uber has settled two major class-action lawsuits with its drivers in California and Massachusetts. The lawsuits challenged the company's business model of classifying drivers as independent contractors rather than employees. The settlement is a significant development in the ongoing debate over the classification of gig economy workers.

Under the terms of the settlement, Uber's drivers will remain independent contractors. The settlement does not provide a specific payout amount, but drivers who have put in more than 25,000 miles for Uber could be paid up to $8,000 each. This development highlights the complexities of the gig economy and the ongoing challenges faced by companies like Uber.
