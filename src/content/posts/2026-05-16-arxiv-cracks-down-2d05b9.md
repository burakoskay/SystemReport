## Introduction to ArXiv's New Policy
ArXiv is taking a significant step to crack down on the misuse of large language models in scientific papers. The research repository will ban authors for a year if they let AI do all the work. This decision reflects a growing concern about the authenticity of research submissions.

The new policy aims to ensure that authors are genuinely contributing to their research, rather than relying solely on AI-generated content. By implementing this ban, ArXiv hopes to maintain the integrity of the research published on its platform. The move is seen as a response to the increasing use of AI tools in academic writing, which has raised questions about authorship and originality.

The use of AI in research has become more prevalent, with many authors using large language models to generate parts of their papers. ArXiv's decision is a direct response to this issue, as the platform seeks to uphold the standards of academic integrity.

The problem of AI-generated research is not new, but it has become more pressing in recent years.
