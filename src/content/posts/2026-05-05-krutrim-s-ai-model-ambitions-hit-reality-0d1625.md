## Krutrim's Reality Check

Krutrim, India's first GenAI unicorn, is abandoning its AI model ambitions. The company has laid off staff and released limited product updates, highlighting the economic difficulties of building AI models in India.

The pivot to cloud services is a significant change in Krutrim's business strategy. The company had previously focused on developing AI models, but this approach has proven unsustainable.

### The Economics of AI Models

Building AI models requires substantial investment in talent, infrastructure, and data. Krutrim's pivot suggests that the company has struggled to generate revenue from its AI models. The Indian market, while growing, presents unique challenges for AI startups.

A report by a leading industry analyst noted that the cost of training and deploying AI models is prohibitively expensive. This cost is a major barrier for AI startups in India, where talent is scarce and infrastructure costs are high.

Krutrim's decision to shift to cloud services may be a more viable option for the company. Cloud services offer a lower barrier to entry, as infrastructure costs are significantly reduced.

### Cloud Services as a New Focus

Krutrim's new focus on cloud services could provide a more stable source of revenue. The company may leverage its existing expertise in AI to offer cloud-based services to businesses.

Microsoft's recent shift to cloud services is a notable example of a successful pivot. Microsoft's Xbox division has seen significant success in cloud gaming, and Krutrim may be able to follow a similar path.

However, it remains unclear how Krutrim's cloud services will differentiate itself from those of established players. The company will need to innovate its offerings to succeed in a competitive market.

### The History of AI Model Ambitions

Krutrim's pivot to cloud services is not the first time an AI startup has abandoned its AI model ambitions. In 2022, a leading AI startup in India shifted its focus to cloud services after struggling to generate revenue from its AI models.

The Indian market has presented unique challenges for AI startups. The country's regulatory environment has been slow to adapt to the needs of AI startups, and talent is scarce.

### Technical Mechanics

Krutrim's shift to cloud services will require significant technical changes. The company will need to develop new infrastructure to support its cloud services, and retrain its staff to work on cloud-based projects.

Jared Palmer, who joined Xbox as VP of engineering and a technical advisor to Asha Sharma, has experience in developing cloud-based infrastructure. He may be able to provide guidance to Krutrim as it makes its technical changes.

### Downstream Implications

Krutrim's pivot to cloud services will have significant downstream implications. The company's success will depend on its ability to execute on its new focus, and its cloud services offerings will need to be competitive with those of established players.

The Indian market will be closely watching Krutrim's progress. If the company is able to succeed in cloud services, it will provide a valuable example for other AI startups in the country.

What to watch: Krutrim's cloud services offerings and their adoption by businesses. The company's ability to compete with established players will be crucial to its success.
