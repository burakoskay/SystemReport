## A New Scheme to Fund the Web

Google has unveiled a new program to let websites collect money from visitors without using online advertising. The Internet is a vast network of websites, and the majority of them rely on advertising to generate revenue. However, this model has several drawbacks, including the collection of personal data and the annoyance of ads.

## A Subscription-Based Model

Google's Contributor program allows internet users to pay a monthly fee to see an ad-free web on participating sites. This scheme has been launched as an experiment with half a dozen partners currently signed up, including Urban Dictionary, The Onion, Science Daily, WikiHow, Mashable, and Imgur.

## How it Works

Participation for internet users is currently limited, though there is a waiting list. Google is offering sign-ups the choice to pay $1, $2, or $3 a month. The more you contribute, the more you support the websites you visit. For now, 'Contributor' will only replace ads provided by Google with a small thank you message.

## A Potential Sea Change

The success of the scheme could encourage more sites to trial subscription services instead of advertising. This would represent a sea change in how the web funds itself, reducing the clout of advertising companies and minimizing the need for sites to collect personal information about their users.

## Broader Industry Context

The technology industry has been slower to adopt a subscription-based model compared to the music industry. Companies like Spotify and Apple Music have seen significant success with their subscription services, offering users access to millions of songs for a flat monthly fee. However, the tech industry has been hesitant to follow suit, with many companies still relying on advertising to generate revenue.

## History of Subscription Services

The concept of subscription-based services is not new, but it has gained momentum in recent years. Companies like Netflix and Amazon Prime have successfully implemented subscription models, offering users exclusive content and benefits. However, the tech industry has been slower to adopt this model, with some companies still relying on advertising revenue.

## Technical Mechanics

The Google Contributor program uses a combination of algorithms and machine learning to determine which websites should be eligible for the ad-free experience. The program also uses a token-based system, where users can purchase tokens that can be redeemed for an ad-free experience on participating sites.

## Downstream Implications

If the Google Contributor program is successful, it could have significant implications for the technology industry. More sites may start to rely on subscription services instead of advertising, reducing the amount of personal data being collected and minimizing the need for annoying ads. However, it could also create new challenges for companies that rely on advertising revenue.
