## iPhone Loyalty Takes a Hit

For the first time since its inception, iPhone owner loyalty has declined in both the United States and Europe. According to research conducted by Strategy Analytics, 88% of U.S. iPhone owners are likely to buy another Apple smartphone, representing a decrease from 93% last year. In Western Europe, the total stands at 75%, which is a decrease from 88% in 2011.

The decline in loyalty is attributed to negative press prompted by a perceived lack of recent innovation by Apple. 'There is no doubt that Apple is continuing its success in retaining existing user base while attracting new customers,' said Paul Brown, director at Strategy Analytics' User Experience Practice. 'However, negative press prompted by a perceived lack of recent innovation by Apple has meant we are starting to see some growth in the number of previously highly loyal consumers who are now reconsidering whether or not they will purchase a new iPhone for their next device.'

This trend is concerning for Apple, as loyalty has been a key factor in the company's success. iPhone owners have historically been more likely to upgrade to new models, and a decline in loyalty could impact future sales.

## Latin America Bucks the Trend

In contrast, Latin America saw a 31% year-over-year surge in iPhone shipments during Q1 2026, driven by strong iPhone 17 performance and an 80% jump in Mexico. According to a new Omdia report, the region's iPhone shipments are on the rise, defying the trend seen in the US and Europe.

The growth in Latin America can be attributed to Apple's continued investment in the region, as well as the popularity of newer iPhone models. The iPhone 17, in particular, has been a strong performer, with its improved specs and features appealing to consumers.

## History of iPhone Loyalty

Historically, iPhone owners have been highly loyal to the brand. According to Strategy Analytics, iPhone owner loyalty has consistently been above 90% in the US and Europe. However, this trend has started to shift, with loyalty declining in recent years.

The decline in loyalty is not limited to the US and Europe. In other regions, such as Asia, iPhone owners are also becoming less loyal. This trend could have significant implications for Apple's future sales and market share.

## Technical Mechanics

The iPhone's popularity can be attributed to its technical capabilities. The device's improved specs and features, such as the A15 Bionic chip and advanced camera system, make it a desirable product for consumers.

However, the iPhone's technical mechanics also contribute to its decline in loyalty. The device's lack of innovation and incremental updates have led to a perception that the iPhone is no longer a groundbreaking product.
