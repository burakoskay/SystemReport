Latitude's new AI-native platform, Voyage, aims to help gamers create their very own role-playing game. Meanwhile, Bond, a new social media platform, wants to use AI to help users kick their doomscrolling habit.

The rise of AI-generated content continues to reshape the tech landscape. Latitude's Voyage platform is designed to empower gamers to create their own RPGs using AI. This move comes as the gaming industry increasingly adopts AI-powered tools to enhance gameplay and user engagement. On the other hand, Bond's AI system is designed to motivate users to engage in activities outside of the app, potentially reducing the negative impacts of excessive social media use.

## Nuclear Power and AI Investments

Blue Energy has raised $380M to build grid-scale nuclear reactors in shipyards, while Anthropic has secured $5B from Amazon and pledged $100B in cloud spending in return. These investments highlight the growing interest in nuclear power and AI.

Blue Energy's approach to building reactors in shipyards aims to reduce costs and increase efficiency. By leveraging shipyard infrastructure, the company hopes to make nuclear power more accessible and affordable. Anthropic's deal with Amazon, on the other hand, underscores the significant investments being made in AI research and development.

## Social Media and AI-Powered Content
