## The Breach

The Internet Archive, a digital library of internet content, has been hit by a catastrophic security breach. On Wednesday afternoon, visitors to the site were greeted with a pop-up message claiming the site had been hacked. The breach was confirmed by Internet Archive founder Brewster Kahle, who said the website had been defaced with the notification via a JavaScript library.

The breach is the latest in a series of attacks on the Internet Archive. In May, the site was DDoS'd, and Jason Scott, an archivist and software curator at the Internet Archive, has previously posted about attacks seemingly aimed at disrupting the site. The current attack appears to be more severe, with the site's operators saying it had been DDoS'd for days.

## The Extent of the Breach

According to Have I Been Pwned (HIBP) operator Troy Hunt, a file containing "email addresses, screen names, password change timestamps, Bcrypt-hashed passwords, and other internal data" for 31 million unique email addresses was leaked. Hunt confirmed the breach was valid by matching data with a user's account. A tweet from HIBP said 54 percent of the accounts were already in its database from previous breaches.

## The Response

The Internet Archive has disabled the JS library, scrubbed its systems, and upgraded security. In a post on X, Kahle confirmed the breach and said the site was experiencing a DDoS attack, which had been fended off for now. An account on X called SN_Blackmeta claimed responsibility for the attack and implied that another attack was planned for the next day.

## The Importance of the Internet Archive

The Internet Archive is a critical part of the internet's infrastructure, providing access to historical content and preserving digital culture. The site's contents include a vast collection of Flash animations, games, and toys, which were preserved using an in-development Flash emulator called Ruffle. The Internet Archive's Emularity system allows users to play a subset of Flash items in the browser as if they had a Flash plugin installed.
