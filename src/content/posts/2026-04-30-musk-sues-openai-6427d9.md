## Introduction to the Lawsuit
Elon Musk is suing OpenAI for breach of contract, alleging the company prioritized profits over the public good. Musk claims OpenAI's shift to a for-profit model was a betrayal of the founding agreement.
Musk's lawsuit accuses OpenAI CEO Sam Altman of fraud and seeks over $134bn in damages, as well as the removal of Altman and OpenAI president Greg Brockman.

## Background on OpenAI and Musk's Involvement
OpenAI was founded in 2015 as a non-profit organization by Musk, Altman, and other Silicon Valley heavyweights. Musk provided $38m in funding, with the goal of developing artificial intelligence in an ethical manner.
However, the relationship between Musk and Altman turned sour in 2017, when Musk grew impatient with OpenAI's progress. Musk eventually left the company's board and began developing his own AI projects.

## The Trial and Its Implications
The trial, which began on Monday, has sizable stakes for OpenAI, which is expected to go public later this year at a $1tn valuation. The case centers around promises and breaches of promises, rather than technical details about artificial intelligence.
Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers has assured prospective jurors that the trial will not be highly technical, despite its focus on AI. Jury selection began with questionnaires and follow-up questions, revealing that many prospective jurors have negative feelings about Musk.
