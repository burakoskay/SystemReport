## Lawmakers Want to Ban AI-Powered Kids' Toys

Connected AI toys are raising questions about children's safety and data protection, prompting some lawmakers to consider an outright ban. These toys, designed to interact with children, learn their preferences, and adapt to their behavior, have sparked concerns about the potential risks they pose. The toys can engage in conversations, create personalized content, and even influence children's behavior, which has led to a surge in the market.

Companies are quickly developing AI-powered toys that utilize natural language processing to converse with children, while others employ machine learning to generate tailored experiences. For instance, some toys use machine learning algorithms to learn children's preferences and adapt to their behavior. This rapid development has led to a surge in the market, with many products available for purchase. However, this also raises concerns about data protection and cybersecurity.
