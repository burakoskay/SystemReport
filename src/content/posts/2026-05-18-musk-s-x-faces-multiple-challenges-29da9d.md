## Introduction to X's Challenges

Elon Musk's X, formerly Twitter, is facing multiple challenges. The platform has been hit with major restrictions on free accounts, and Musk is being sued by Don Lemon for fraud and breach of contract. Additionally, X is struggling to remove content related to a Sydney stabbing, and the Australian government is calling for stronger laws governing social media platforms. X's content moderation issues have been a point of contention, with the Australian eSafety commissioner issuing a notice to X and Meta to remove material that depicted gratuitous or offensive violence.

## X's Content Moderation Issues

X has been accused of not doing enough to remove content related to the Sydney stabbing. The Australian eSafety commissioner issued a notice to X and Meta to remove material that depicted gratuitous or offensive violence. While X claims to have complied with the directive, the commissioner's office has made multiple attempts to ensure X had been fully compliant. Musk has responded by saying that the Australian censorship commissar is demanding global content bans. This issue highlights the challenges X faces in balancing free speech with the need to remove harmful content.

## Tesla's Involvement with X

Tesla is paying $20,000 per year to be on X, with 13 affiliated accounts requiring an additional $50 per month payment. The automaker has launched several new accounts on X since Musk took over, including a new Tesla North America account, a new Tesla Europe account, and a new Tesla AI account. This strategy is unique to X, as Tesla operates only a single official account on other social media platforms. Furthermore, Tesla's purchase of unsold Cybertrucks from itself raises questions about the company's decision-making and the financial health of the electric pickup truck program.

## Lawsuits Against X

Don Lemon has sued Elon Musk and X for fraud and breach of contract over an axed show. The lawsuit claims that Musk and X lured Lemon into an exclusive show deal promising $1.5 million annually, full authority over his content, and financial incentives. However, Musk and X pulled out of the deal without payment. The complaint alleges that Musk and X induced Lemon to enter into an exclusive partnership deal with false promises and representations.
