## Smart Home Devices Get Smarter

The smart home market is rapidly evolving, with new devices and standards emerging to make homes more connected and convenient. One of the latest additions is the Ulticam IQ V2, a Matter-certified AI security camera that boasts an impressive list of features, including Power over Ethernet, 4K resolution, and Google Gemini AI integrations. This device is significant because it is one of the first to be certified under the Matter standard, which aims to improve interoperability between smart home devices.

The Ulticam IQ V2 is not just a simple security camera; it is a powerful tool that can be used to monitor and manage a smart home. With its AI capabilities, it can detect and alert homeowners to potential security threats, and its integration with other smart devices means that it can be used to control lighting, thermostats, and other appliances. For example, the camera can be integrated with smart lighting systems to turn on lights when motion is detected, or with smart thermostats to adjust the temperature when someone enters or leaves the home.

## The Rise of Smart Home Standards

The emergence of smart home standards like Matter is a significant development in the industry. For years, smart home devices have been plagued by compatibility issues, with different devices and systems often unable to communicate with each other. The Matter standard aims to change that by providing a common language that devices can use to communicate with each other. This will make it easier for homeowners to integrate different devices and systems into their smart home, and will also make it easier for device manufacturers to develop products that are compatible with a wide range of systems.

The Zigbee Alliance is also working on a new standard, called Project Connected Home over IP, which aims to improve interoperability between smart home devices. The project has already gained significant traction, with over 145 active member companies and hundreds of product, engineering, and marketing experts working on the standard. The goal is to create a draft specification by the end of 2020 and release the completed standard in 2021.
