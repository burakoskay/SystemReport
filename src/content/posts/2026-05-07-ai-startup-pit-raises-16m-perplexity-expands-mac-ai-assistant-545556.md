## AI Startup Pit Secures $16M in Funding

Pit, a new AI startup led by the cofounders of European scooter giant Voi, has raised $16 million in a seed round led by a16z. The company aims to make a name for itself in the competitive AI landscape. This significant investment is a vote of confidence in Pit's potential to disrupt the AI industry.

The funding round was secured due to the company's potential for growth and innovation in the AI sector. However, details about the startup's specific projects and timelines remain scarce.

## Perplexity Expands Personal Computer AI Assistant to All Mac Users

Perplexity's Personal Computer AI assistant is now available to all Mac users, no longer limited to those with a Max subscription. The AI assistant brings AI agents directly to users' Macs, allowing for native interaction.

The move expands Perplexity's reach and puts its AI technology in front of a broader audience. Users can now interact with AI agents natively on their Macs, which could lead to increased user adoption and feedback. This expansion may also drive growth and revenue for Perplexity.

## Distr 2.0 Streamlines Software Distribution for AI Companies

Distr, an open-source software distribution platform, has released version 2.0. The platform enables software and AI companies to distribute applications to self-managed customers with minimal setup. Distr provides prebuilt components for easy deployment in complex environments.

The platform is designed to simplify the distribution process, allowing companies to focus on their core products. Distr's growth will depend on its ability to attract more software and AI companies to its platform. The release of Distr 2.0 marks a significant milestone in the company's development.
