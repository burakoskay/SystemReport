## Trust and Guardrails in AI

Barry Diller's recent comments on AGI highlight the need for guardrails as the technology advances. Diller trusts Sam Altman, but notes that 'trust is irrelevant' as AGI nears, emphasizing the unpredictable nature of this technology. This sentiment is particularly relevant given the recent developments in AI deals and partnerships.

The termination of Snap's $400 million deal with Perplexity, an AI search engine company, underscores the complexities of integrating AI into existing platforms. The deal, which would have integrated Perplexity's AI search engine into Snapchat, was 'amicably ended', leaving questions about the future of AI search engines in social media. This move has sparked discussions about the viability of AI search engines in social media platforms and the challenges of integrating these technologies.

The end of the deal also raises questions about Perplexity's business model and its ability to secure partnerships with major tech companies. As the AI landscape continues to evolve, companies like Perplexity will need to adapt and innovate to remain competitive.

## AI Deals and Computing Resources

The demand for computing resources to support AI development is growing rapidly. Anthropic's deal with SpaceX to use computing resources from Elon Musk's xAI is a testament to this trend. This partnership not only highlights the need for significant computing power but also the strategic alliances being formed in the AI race.

Anthropic has also raised usage limits for its Claude Code model and signed deals with Microsoft, Amazon, and other companies to access computing resources. These moves indicate a broader strategy to expand AI capabilities and meet the increasing demand for AI computing resources. The company's decision to raise usage limits and secure additional computing resources demonstrates its commitment to developing more advanced AI systems.

The increasing demand for computing resources has significant implications for the AI industry. As companies like Anthropic and OpenAI continue to develop more complex AI models, they will require substantial computing power to support their efforts. This has led to a surge in demand for specialized hardware and infrastructure designed specifically for AI workloads.

## Regulatory Scrutiny and AI Development

Regulatory challenges are becoming a critical aspect of AI development. Canadian officials have accused OpenAI of violating federal and provincial privacy laws, citing excessive personal data collection and inadequate consent. This accusation underscores the importance of data privacy and consent in the development of AI systems.

OpenAI must navigate these regulatory challenges as it develops more advanced AI systems. The outcome of these regulatory decisions will significantly impact the trajectory of the industry, making it crucial to track developments in AGI guardrails, AI computing resources, and data collection practices. The company's ability to address regulatory concerns and adapt to changing requirements will be essential in shaping the future of AI.

The regulatory scrutiny faced by OpenAI highlights the need for more transparent and accountable AI development practices. As AI systems become increasingly pervasive, companies must prioritize data privacy and consent to maintain public trust and avoid regulatory repercussions.
