## Conversational AI Lands in Gmail

Google has started rolling out a conversational AI feature to its Gmail inbox, allowing users to interact with their emails using natural language. This means you can now ask Gemini, Google's AI model, to find specific details buried in your inbox without having to manually search for them.

The feature, which is powered by Gemini, allows users to ask questions like "Show me all the emails from John about the project" or "What was the last email from Jane about?" and get relevant results. This is a significant improvement over traditional search functionality, which often requires users to know exactly what they're looking for and use specific keywords.

## How it Works

According to TechCrunch, the conversational AI feature in Gmail works by allowing users to type in natural language queries, which are then interpreted by Gemini. The AI model can understand context and follow up questions, making it easier for users to dig deeper into their emails. For example, if you ask Gemini to show you all the emails from a specific sender, you can then ask follow-up questions like "What was the last email about?" or "Show me only the emails from yesterday."

The technical mechanics behind Google's conversational AI feature in Gmail are based on its Gemini model, which is a large language model capable of understanding natural language queries.
