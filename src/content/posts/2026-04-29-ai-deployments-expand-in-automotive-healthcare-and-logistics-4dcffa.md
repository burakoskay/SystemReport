## General Motors to Bring Gemini AI to Four Million Vehicles

General Motors is planning to integrate Google's Gemini AI assistant into around four million vehicles across the US. The rollout will cover model year 2022 and newer Cadillac, Chevrolet, Buick, and GMC vehicles with Google built-in, via over-the-air software updates for GM's infotainment system over several months. This update represents one of the largest deployments of Gemini in the industry, according to GM.

The AI upgrade will provide customers with a smarter, more intuitive AI assistant that continues to improve over time. 

## AI in Healthcare: Antibiotic Resistance and Diagnostics

At WIRED Health, British surgeon Ara Darzi mentioned that AI is set to transform the diagnosis and treatment of drug-resistant infections. However, a lack of incentives may hinder innovation from reaching patients. 

The use of AI in healthcare, particularly in diagnostics and treatment, is becoming increasingly prominent. For instance, AI can help analyze medical data to identify potential antibiotic-resistant bacteria, allowing for more targeted and effective treatment. Moreover, AI-powered diagnostic tools can help healthcare professionals identify diseases more accurately and quickly.

The integration of AI in healthcare also raises questions about data privacy and security. As AI systems rely on vast amounts of sensitive patient data, it is crucial to ensure that this data is protected from unauthorized access.

## Logistics and Supply Chain Management

Flexport, a logistics company, has unveiled its latest AI-powered innovations to modernize global supply chains. The company has enhanced its core platforms to provide unmatched visibility and control from factory floor to customer door. 

A single, centralized platform manages logistics providers and suppliers, covering order management, booking management, allocation, and supply chain optimization. This platform enables real-time tracking and monitoring of shipments, allowing for more efficient and responsive logistics management.

The use of AI in logistics can also help reduce costs and improve delivery times. By optimizing routes and streamlining supply chains, companies can save money and improve customer satisfaction.

## Autonomous Systems and Military Applications

Scout AI has raised $100 million to train its models for war, with a focus on developing AI agents that give individual soldiers control of fleets of autonomous vehicles. 

The company's training ground, or 'bootcamp,' is working on advanced AI systems for military applications. This includes the development of AI-powered autonomous vehicles that can operate in complex and dynamic environments.

The use of AI in military applications raises questions about the potential risks and benefits. While AI-powered autonomous systems can improve military effectiveness and reduce the risk of human casualties, they also raise concerns about accountability and control.

## Job Market and AI Training

Over 700 people working for a Meta contractor in Ireland are at risk of losing their jobs as the company considers layoffs. 

The workers are involved in training Meta's AI models. This highlights the potential impact of AI on the job market, as automation and AI-powered systems increasingly take over tasks previously performed by humans.
