## AI Upgrades to Camera and Photos
Apple is enhancing the iPhone camera with AI-powered editing tools. According to 9to5Mac, these upgrades build on the redesign introduced last year with iOS 26. The new tools will include a broader set of AI-enhanced editing tools in the Photos app. This move could simplify photo editing for power users, allowing them to make more precise adjustments to their images.

The camera AI could also enable more advanced features such as automatic object removal, skin smoothing, and noise reduction. These features would be particularly useful for photographers who want to edit their photos on the go. The AI-powered editing tools will also learn the user's editing style over time, making it easier to apply consistent edits to multiple photos.

## Keyboard Gets Alternative-Word Suggestions
Mark Gurman reported that Apple is testing a keyboard that expands autocorrect by offering alternative words. This feature works like a lightweight Grammarly, surfacing synonyms or phrasing tweaks as you type. The smarter keyboard promises to reduce the mental load of phrasing by suggesting alternative words, making it easier for users to express themselves.

The keyboard upgrade could also include features such as predictive text and personalized suggestions based on the user's writing style. This would make it easier for users to type quickly and accurately, without having to worry about spelling mistakes or grammar errors. The keyboard could also learn the user's language preferences over time, making it easier to switch between languages.

## Siri Steps Out as Its Own App
Gurman's Power On newsletter revealed a dedicated Siri app for iOS 27, iPadOS 27, and macOS 27. The app will let users converse with Siri in both text and voice modes and includes an 'Extensions' feature. The Siri app will function similarly to the ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude apps, providing users with access to their past conversations with Siri.

The Siri app could also enable more advanced features such as multi-step conversations, follow-up questions, and contextual understanding. These features would make it easier for users to interact with Siri, allowing them to have more natural and intuitive conversations. The Siri app could also integrate with other Apple services, such as Maps and Calendar, to provide more personalized and relevant results.
