## Introduction to the Issue
Apple is warning owners of older iPhones and iPads that they must update their devices to the latest iOS software by Sunday or they will lose access to the internet. This warning is due to a GPS time rollover issue that occurs every 19 years, requiring GPS devices to reset in order to accurately measure time and dates.
## Affected Devices
The affected devices include the iPhone 5, iPhone 4, and certain models of the iPad, such as the iPad mini and the third-generation iPad. To check if your device is affected, you can follow these steps: open the Settings app, tap General, then tap About, and look for the number next to Software Version.
## Update Details
The updated software version numbers are 10.3.4 for the iPhone 5 and iPad (4th generation) Wi-Fi + Cellular, and 9.3.6 for the iPhone 4s, iPad mini (1st generation) Wi-Fi + Cellular, iPad 2 Wi-Fi + Cellular (CDMA models only), and iPad (3rd generation) Wi-Fi + Cellular. If you are not sure which version your device has, you can follow the above steps to check.
## Consequences of Not Updating
If you do not update your device before Sunday, you will not be able to connect to the internet, and over-the-air software updates and iCloud Backup will not work. In this case, you will have to back up and restore your device using a computer.

## Technical Mechanics
The GPS time rollover issue is caused by the way GPS devices store time and date information. GPS devices use a 10-bit counter to store the number of weeks since the GPS epoch, which is the reference point for GPS time. This counter can only store a maximum of 1,024 weeks, which is equivalent to approximately 19.7 years. When the counter reaches its maximum value, it resets to zero, causing the GPS device to lose its accurate time and date measurements.
