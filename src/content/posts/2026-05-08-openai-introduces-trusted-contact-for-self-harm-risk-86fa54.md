## OpenAI introduces Trusted Contact for self-harm risk

OpenAI announced that users can now nominate a Trusted Contact who will receive a warning if the system detects self-harm risk. The feature embeds a prompt that asks the model to flag language indicating suicidal ideation. This move comes as the company faces increasing scrutiny over its handling of user safety, particularly for vulnerable populations such as minors.

The Trusted Contact feature is a significant step towards mitigating the risks associated with AI chatbots, especially in cases where users may be struggling with mental health issues. By allowing users to nominate a trusted individual who can receive alerts in case of self-harm risk, OpenAI aims to provide an additional layer of support and protection.

## Senate advances the GUARD Act to curb AI companions for minors

The Senate Judiciary Committee unanimously advanced the GUARD Act (S.3062) on April 30, 2026. Senators Josh Hawley and Richard Blumenthal introduced the bill on October 28, 2025. The legislation would require age verification for any service marketed as an “AI companion” and would bar minors from chatbots that simulate friendship or therapeutic interaction.

The GUARD Act is a response to growing concerns about the impact of AI chatbots on minors. The bill's sponsors argue that age verification and restrictions on AI companions are necessary to protect children from potential harm. However, critics argue that the bill could have unintended consequences, such as driving users to unverified alternatives.

## Industry grapples with verification and safety engineering

Age verification at scale mixes government ID checks, third-party services, and behavioral signals. Implementing it for conversational agents introduces friction that could drive users to unverified alternatives. The challenge lies in striking a balance between safety and usability.

Companies like OpenAI are exploring various approaches to verification and safety engineering. For instance, OpenAI's Trusted Contact feature relies on machine learning models to detect language indicating suicidal ideation.
