The surge of handcrafted purse computers on TikTok shows makers reclaiming control over their digital lives. These cyberdecks, built by young women and posted on the platform, are functional computers that fit inside a handbag and offer a tactile experience of computing.

At the same time, AI-powered accessories like SpeakON are gaining attention. The device attaches magnetically to the iPhone's rear, listens to speech, and refines transcription according to user-defined preferences. According to recent testing, SpeakON improves accuracy for technical jargon.

The cyberdeck trend is a statement about control and transparency in computing. By building their own hardware, creators can understand and modify their devices without relying on proprietary firmware. This movement echoes the home-brew computer clubs of the 1970s, where enthusiasts built machines from kits and shared schematics.

The cyberdeck has been dubbed 'the hottest anti-AI gadget' by some, highlighting a growing interest in hardware that values transparency over convenience. Meanwhile, AI accessories like SpeakON represent a different approach, one that leverages AI to enhance productivity.

Apple's rumored MacBook Ultra adds another layer to the discussion. If the rumor holds, the Ultra could become the default platform for developers who need sustained performance. However, this may also mean a more closed system that relies heavily on Apple's own silicon and software stack.

## The Rise of DIY Computing
The DIY computing movement is not new, but its resurgence on TikTok and other social media platforms is notable. The platform's short-form video format allows creators to showcase their projects in a concise and engaging way. The cyberdeck trend is also a testament to the creativity and resourcefulness of young makers.

## A Brief History of DIY Computing
The home-brew computer clubs of the 1970s laid the groundwork for the modern DIY computing movement. Enthusiasts built machines from kits and shared schematics, fostering a sense of community and collaboration. The cyberdeck trend is a nod to this history, with creators building and sharing their own custom computers.

## Technical Mechanics
The cyberdecks built by TikTok creators are often based on open-source hardware and software. This allows them to customize and modify their devices to suit their needs. The use of open-source components also enables creators to understand and learn from the inner workings of their devices.
