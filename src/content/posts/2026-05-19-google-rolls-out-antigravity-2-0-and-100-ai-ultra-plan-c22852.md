Google launched Antigravity 2.0 and raised the price of its top‑tier AI Ultra plan to $100 a month. The move doubles the headline price of Google’s most expensive AI subscription while promising five times the usage limit of the AI Pro tier.

The AI Ultra plan now costs $100 per month and delivers five‑fold the usage allowance of the AI Pro tier, according to TechCrunch. The same announcement introduced Antigravity 2.0 as a refreshed desktop application and command‑line interface, a pairing that Google markets as the primary gateway to its generative AI services.

## Antigravity 2.0: what changes

Antigravity 2.0 arrives as a new desktop client and a command‑line tool that replace the previous generation. The desktop app offers a graphical front‑end for prompt entry, response viewing, and token management. The CLI gives developers a scriptable path to the same back‑end, enabling automation in CI pipelines and local testing.

Google frames the update as a tighter integration with its cloud‑hosted models. By exposing the same API keys through both interfaces, users can switch between a point‑and‑click workflow and a terminal‑driven one without changing credentials. The design removes a friction point that many developers complained about in earlier releases.

## Pricing shift and usage limits

The AI Ultra plan’s $100 price tag replaces the previous $120 figure mentioned in earlier rumors, making the tier “a little cheaper,” as Engadget noted. The plan’s headline benefit is a five‑times higher usage ceiling than the AI Pro tier, which sits at a lower price point.

Google does not disclose the exact token count attached to each tier, but the ratio signals a clear tiered strategy: casual users stay on the free or Pro plans, while power users who need sustained throughput move to Ultra. The pricing adjustment nudges the market ceiling upward while still undercutting comparable offerings from rival providers.

## How the move fits Google’s AI pricing history

Google has been reshaping its AI subscription model for the past 18 months. The company introduced a free tier, a mid‑range Pro tier, and the Ultra tier as part of a broader effort to monetize Gemini‑based models. Earlier this year the Pro plan sat at $20 per month, a figure that has remained stable.

The latest price cut on the Ultra tier suggests Google is testing elasticity at the high end. By lowering the top‑end price while expanding the usage quota, the company hopes to capture enterprises that were hesitant to cross the $120 barrier. The strategy mirrors a pattern seen in cloud compute pricing, where volume discounts encourage larger contracts.

## Competitive pressure from OpenAI and Anthropic

OpenAI’s ChatGPT Enterprise plan currently costs $20 per user per month and promises unlimited usage. Anthropic’s Claude 3 offering charges $30 per month for a comparable quota. Google’s $100 Ultra plan sits above these headline prices, but the five‑fold usage boost could justify the premium for workloads that demand massive token throughput.

The competitive field is crowded with startups offering niche pricing models, such as per‑token billing or usage‑based discounts. Google’s tiered approach bets on brand trust and integration depth rather than pure price competition. The Antigravity 2.0 tooling also aims to lock developers into the Google ecosystem, a tactic that rivals have struggled to replicate.

## What to watch

The next quarter will reveal whether the $100 Ultra plan attracts enough high‑volume users to offset the lower price point. Analysts will track subscription uptake, token consumption trends, and any subsequent price tweaks. The rollout of Antigravity 2.0’s CLI will also be a litmus test for developer adoption; a surge in GitHub activity around the tool could signal a shift in workflow preferences. Keep an eye on Google’s quarterly earnings call for the first hard data on Ultra‑plan churn and on any announced enhancements to the Antigravity suite.
