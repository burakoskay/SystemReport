## The Unseen Story Behind Cholesterol Tests

Millions of Americans undergo cholesterol tests every year, but these tests may not be telling the whole story. A more accurate method of measuring cholesterol risk has been available for years, yet it remains underused by doctors.

The standard cholesterol test measures levels of low-density lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol, often referred to as "bad" cholesterol. However, this test does not account for the size and density of LDL particles, which are also crucial factors in determining cardiovascular risk. A newer test, called apolipoprotein B (apoB) testing, provides a more accurate picture of cholesterol risk by measuring the number of atherogenic particles in the blood.

## Why ApoB Testing Matters

ApoB testing has been shown to be a better predictor of cardiovascular risk than traditional cholesterol testing. This is because apoB testing measures the number of atherogenic particles, including LDL and very-low-density lipoprotein (VLDL) particles, which are the primary drivers of plaque buildup in arteries. In contrast, traditional cholesterol testing only measures the levels of LDL cholesterol, which can be misleading.

For example, a person with high levels of LDL cholesterol may not necessarily have a high number of atherogenic particles if they have large, buoyant LDL particles. Conversely, a person with low levels of LDL cholesterol may still have a high number of atherogenic particles if they have small, dense LDL particles. ApoB testing can help identify individuals who are at high risk of cardiovascular disease, even if their traditional cholesterol test results appear normal.
