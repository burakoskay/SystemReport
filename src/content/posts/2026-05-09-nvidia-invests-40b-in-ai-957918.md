## Introduction to Nvidia's AI Investments

Nvidia has committed $40B to equity AI deals this year. This investment is a significant move in the AI ecosystem, reflecting the company's strategy to play a central role in the development of artificial intelligence technologies.

Nvidia's investment is part of the larger trend of big tech companies investing in AI. Other companies like Meta, Google, and Amazon are also investing heavily in AI development, focusing on areas such as natural language processing, computer vision, and generative AI.

## The Role of Underwater Cables in AI Development

Over 95% of international data and voice call traffic travels through nearly a million miles of underwater communication cables. These cables carry government communications, financial transactions, email, video calls, and streaming around the world, serving as the backbone of global connectivity.

The first commercial telecommunication subsea cable was used for telegraphs and was laid across the English Channel between Dover, England, and Calais, France in 1850. The technology then evolved to coaxial cables that carried telephone conversations, and most recently, fiber optics that ferry data and the internet as we know it. The development of underwater cables has been crucial in enabling the rapid exchange of information across continents.

## Big Tech's Investment in Underwater Cables

Big tech companies are investing in underwater cables to support their AI development. Meta, for example, has announced Project Waterworth, a 50,000km (31,000-mile) cable that will connect five continents, making it the world's longest subsea cable project. This investment reflects Meta's commitment to enhancing global connectivity and supporting the growth of AI applications.

Amazon has also announced its first wholly-owned subsea cable project called Fastnet. Fastnet will connect Maryland's eastern shore to County Cork, Ireland, and capacity will exceed 320 terabits per second, which is equivalent to streaming 12.5 million HD movies simultaneously. This project highlights Amazon's focus on building robust infrastructure to support its AI and cloud computing services.

## Meta's Generative AI Investments

Meta's investments in AI are coming along four key areas: Llama to power the AI features in their products, Meta AI, Core Recommendations and Ads, and AI in Reality Labs Devices. By focusing on these areas, Meta aims to integrate AI across its product suite, enhancing user experience and driving engagement.

Meta continues to invest in open source, with the launch of Llama 3.2 models this past quarter, including the best performing open source multimodal models. This move underscores Meta's commitment to advancing the field of AI through open collaboration and innovation.
