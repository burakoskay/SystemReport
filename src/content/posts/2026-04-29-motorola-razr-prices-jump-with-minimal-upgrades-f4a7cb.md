## Price hike on all three Razr flip phones
Motorola announced a price increase across its 2026 Razr family. The move comes with minimal hardware revisions. According to the company, the 2026 Razr phones get "some new — and, as you'd expect, more expensive — prices." This decision may seem counterintuitive, given that the upgrades are not substantial. The price hike may affect the device's competitiveness in the market, especially considering the strong offerings from competitors.

## What's new in the 2026 lineup
The company lists minor adjustments: a slightly tweaked UI, marginally brighter display settings, and a new color option for the flagship model. No major processor upgrade, camera overhaul, or battery increase is mentioned. The only tangible change that could matter to power users is the addition of a new colorway that mirrors the original 2004 Razr's chrome finish. This color option may appeal to nostalgic consumers who valued the original Razr's design.

The foldable phone market has seen significant growth in recent years, with several manufacturers competing for market share. ## Technical mechanics: What drives the price increase?
The minor upgrades in the 2026 Razr lineup may not justify the price increase, according to some critics. ## Moto Buds 2 Plus arrive with Bose sound
Ahead of the Razr refresh, Motorola introduced the Moto Buds 2 Plus, pricing them at $149 in the United States. The earbuds feature Bose-engineered audio drivers. This new product launch may be an attempt by Motorola to diversify its offerings and attract a wider range of customers. The Moto Buds 2 Plus may appeal to consumers seeking high-quality audio accessories, which could help Motorola expand its customer base.
