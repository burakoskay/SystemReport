## The Unintended Consequences of YouTube's Algorithm

YouTube's search and recommendation algorithms are driving children to Russian-language content even when they seek out videos in Kyrgyz, creating a cultural shift that concerns some parents. This phenomenon highlights the challenges of navigating language and cultural diversity in online platforms.

## The Impact on Language and Culture

The spread of Russian-language content on YouTube is not an isolated incident. Research has shown that language and culture play a significant role in shaping our perceptions and understanding of abstract concepts. A study published in the journal *Nature* found that abstract concepts are represented in the brain across cultures and languages, suggesting that there is a universal neural infrastructure for thinking about abstract ideas.[^1][^2][^3][^4] For instance, the study revealed that people from different cultural backgrounds and languages tend to form abstract concepts in the same brain regions.[^1][^3]

The database of places, language, culture, and environment, known as D-Place, also supports this notion. It aims to bring together a dispersed corpus of information on cultural practices, making it easier for individuals to contrast their own cultural practices with those of others.[^5][^6][^7][^8] This resource could be invaluable in understanding the implications of language and cultural diversity on online platforms.

## A Growing Trend: Language-Specific AI Models

In response to the dominance of large language models (LLMs) developed by American and Chinese tech giants, smaller nations are building their own language-specific AI models. For example, Egune AI, a startup based in Mongolia, is developing LLMs that specialize in Mongolia's language, culture, and nomadic traditions. This movement aims to reduce reliance on foreign tech giants and safeguard local language and cultural identity.

Badral Sanlig, the founder of Egune AI, is building a large language model with only 128 GPUs and a small team of engineers to serve just Mongolia. This approach highlights the resourcefulness and adaptability of smaller nations in the face of technological advancements. By developing their own language-specific AI models, these nations can ensure that their unique cultural and linguistic heritage is preserved and promoted.

## The Broader Industry Context

The development of language-specific AI models and the spread of Russian-language content on YouTube are part of a larger trend. As global migration and online learning become increasingly common, it is essential to consider the cultural and language background of learners and users. This requires careful planning and design of online content to avoid misleading information and ensure that knowledge is disseminated in its original form.

The importance of cultural and language diversity in online learning environments cannot be overstated. A study on culture and language diversity in online learning environments emphasized the need for careful planning when designing online courses, especially those expected to be accessed by international learners. The authors noted that global migration has led to a substantial population of immigrants or expatriates with unique traditions, ethnicity, religions, and cultures.
