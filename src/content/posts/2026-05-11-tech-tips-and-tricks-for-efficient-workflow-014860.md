## Mastering Keyboard Shortcuts

Every time my cat walks across a keyboard, I learn a few new Mac and PC keyboard shortcuts I never knew about. It's amazing how often these shortcuts can save time and increase productivity. For instance, using keyboard shortcuts can help you navigate through documents and applications more efficiently.

On a Mac, you can use `Command + Tab` to switch between open applications, while on a PC, you can use `Alt + Tab` for the same purpose. These shortcuts might seem simple, but they can greatly impact your workflow.

One of the most useful keyboard shortcuts I've learned is `Ctrl + Shift + T` on Windows or `Command + Shift + T` on Mac, which reopens a closed tab in most browsers. This shortcut has saved me countless times when I accidentally close a tab.

In addition to these basic shortcuts, there are many more advanced ones that can help you work more efficiently. For example, on a Mac, you can use `Command + Shift + 4` to take a screenshot of a selected area, or `Command + Shift + 5` to take a screenshot of the entire screen.

## Handy Tools for Tech Enthusiasts

A collection of tips and tricks from interactive installations reveals the importance of having a few essential tools on hand. The Flipper Zero, a hacker's multi-tool, is great for testing sensors, triggering environmental controls, and red-teaming your system. A spare Ethernet cable can stand in for a bad one, connect to a network while waiting for Wi-Fi, and more.

A Raspberry Pi, especially the Pi 5, is a versatile device that can act as a spare exhibit server, a VPN/jumpbox, or a test/debug server. The Pi Zero is also quite capable and inexpensive enough to keep one or two in your bag. Having a capable multitool like the Leatherman Wingman or Victorinox Spirit can come in handy in unexpected situations.

For instance, I once used a Raspberry Pi to set up a temporary network for a project. It was easy to set up and allowed me to test my application without relying on a traditional network. The Raspberry Pi is a great example of a tool that can be used in a variety of situations, from testing and debugging to providing a temporary solution for a project.

## Strategies for Efficient Workflow

A robust and efficient logging strategy can make a world of difference when monitoring the health of an exhibit or trying to deduce what went wrong. Having a bootable USB drive on hand can be a lifesaver when provisioning machines, rescuing broken machines, or resizing hard drive partitions.

A sneakernet, or the ability to copy files between air-gapped machines, can be critical to adhering to a project's timeline. This method allows you to transfer files without relying on a network connection, making it a valuable asset in certain situations.

For example, I once worked on a project where we had to transfer large files between machines that were not connected to the network. We used a sneakernet to transfer the files, which saved us a significant amount of time. The sneakernet is a great example of a strategy that can be used to improve workflow efficiency.
