## Spatial wallpapers reshape the home screen

iOS 26 now lets you turn the lock screen into a 3D spatial environment. The feature projects depth onto the background as you tilt the device, making icons appear to float in a miniature world. This effect is available in the initial release, and it activates automatically when you select a supported wallpaper.

To enable it, open Settings → Wallpaper, tap + to add a new image, and look for the ‘Spatial’ badge. Once applied, the system uses the phone’s accelerometer and gyroscope to shift the scene in real time.

## CarPlay gains two user-focused features

CarPlay’s evolution under iOS 26 continues the trend of squeezing more utility into the dashboard. Two fresh features landed in the latest update, both aimed at reducing driver distraction. One adds a persistent quick-access button for the most-used third-party music app, letting you swap streams without reaching for the phone. The other introduces a voice-triggered shortcut that launches a navigation route to ‘home’ with a single command, bypassing the need to tap through the map UI.

## The broader industry context

iOS 26 does not exist in a vacuum. The last year saw iOS 18 arrive on September 16, bringing icon-customization, lock-screen shortcut swaps, a redesigned Control Center, RCS support, and a dedicated Passwords app. Those tweaks were incremental, but they set a tone of personalization that iOS 26 now amplifies with 3D spatial wallpapers. The CarPlay features also build on the trend of making in-car experiences more seamless and user-friendly. Other car manufacturers are working on similar features, such as Ford’s SYNC system, which offers a range of customization options and voice-activated controls.

## The history: prior launches, precedent regulatory actions, earlier incidents of the same category

This update also brought other features, such as ARKit 1.5 and Core ML 2. The company has been gradually refining its approach to personalization, starting with iOS 7’s top-down redesign.

## Technical mechanics behind spatial wallpapers and CarPlay integration

Spatial wallpapers rely on a combination of Core Animation layers and motion-sensor data. When you select a supported image, the system creates a depth map or generates one algorithmically. The GPU then composites the foreground and background layers while applying a parallax offset derived from the device’s accelerometer.
