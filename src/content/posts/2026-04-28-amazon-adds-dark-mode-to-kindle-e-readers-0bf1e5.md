## Amazon's Kindle Dark Mode Update

Amazon today announced a software update for its Kindle Colorsoft and Kindle Scribe Colorsoft e-readers, bringing dark mode to both devices. The update allows users to toggle dark mode for specific menus, such as library pages, while keeping note-taking areas bright. This move addresses a long-standing omission in Amazon's e-reader lineup, as dark mode has been available on other Kindle devices.

The addition of dark mode is a significant enhancement, particularly for users who prefer reading in low-light environments. By providing this feature, Amazon is improving the overall user experience and making its e-readers more competitive in the market.

## Google Wallet's ID Expansion

Google Wallet is expanding digital ID support to India, extending a feature that has been slowly rolling out across US states and select passports. This move positions Google's wallet as a universal credential hub, potentially reducing reliance on physical cards. The expansion of digital ID support in Google Wallet has significant implications for the future of mobile payments and identity verification.

By entering the Indian market, Google Wallet is capitalizing on the country's growing digital economy. This strategic move enables Google to establish a strong presence in a key market and sets the stage for further expansion in the region.
