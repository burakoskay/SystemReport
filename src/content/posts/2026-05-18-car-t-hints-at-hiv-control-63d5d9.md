Tech breakthroughs and controversies collided this week as a CAR‑T HIV study reported early signals, a firearms trainer faced murder probes, BMW released a manual M3 CS, and Marvel added new heroes to its beat‑em‑up.

The HIV trial repurposed a CAR‑T protocol that already treats certain cancers, the ICE trainer is run by former Phoenix officer David Norman, the 2027 M3 CS Handschalter arrives with a three‑pedal layout, and Marvel’s Cosmic Invasion now lets players control Cyclops and The Thing.

## CAR‑T Therapy Takes a Shot at HIV

WIRED noted that CAR‑T cell therapy has cured blood cancers by reprogramming a patient’s T cells. The same engineering now targets the latent HIV reservoir. The study described by WIRED enrolled a handful of participants and reported measurable reductions in viral load without antiretroviral drugs.

The result is not a cure, but it shows that engineered immune cells can keep the virus in check. Experts who follow CAR‑T research call the data “promising but preliminary.” The trial’s size prevents any claim about population‑level impact. Still, the approach sidesteps the lifelong pill regimen that defines current HIV care.

If larger trials replicate the findings, the HIV field could gain a new therapeutic class. For now, the data sit alongside decades of effort to eradicate the virus from the body’s hidden reservoirs.

## ICE Trainer Linked to Deadly Shootings

WIRED uncovered that a firearms trainer tied to at least four lethal incidents was run by David Norman, a former Phoenix police officer who describes himself as “a fucking savage.” Norman’s company supplied tactical instruction to Homeland Security’s Special Response Teams.

The investigation linked the trainer’s curriculum to the tactics used in each shooting. Law‑enforcement observers argue that the trainer’s emphasis on lethal force contributed to the outcomes. Norman’s past as a police officer gives the program credibility within federal circles, yet the fatal incidents have sparked calls for oversight.

Critics demand that agencies audit the content of contracted trainers. The Department of Homeland Security has not yet responded to requests for comment. The controversy may force a review of how private tactical instruction is vetted for government contracts.

## BMW Rolls Out Manual‑Gear M3 CS

Ars Technica reported that BMW’s 2027 M3 CS Handschalter arrives with a manual gearbox and rear‑wheel drive, a rarity in a market dominated by dual‑clutch automatics. The model sheds weight compared with the standard M3 and adds a third pedal, restoring the clutch to the driver’s workflow.

The decision signals BMW’s commitment to the enthusiast segment that still values driver engagement. The lighter chassis and reduced drivetrain losses translate into sharper cornering and a more audible engine experience. The M3 CS Handschalter will ship with a six‑speed gearbox that pairs with the M‑Power engine’s high‑revving character.

Enthusiasts who have long complained about the loss of manual options see the CS as a corrective move. The model’s limited production run is expected to keep demand high, though pricing details remain undisclosed.

## Marvel Expands Cosmic Invasion Roster

Engadget confirmed that Marvel’s retro‑inspired beat‑em‑up, Cosmic Invasion, now includes Cyclops and The Thing as playable characters. The update arrives as part of a broader content push aimed at revitalizing the game’s multiplayer loops.

Cyclops brings a ranged optic blast that forces opponents to dodge, while The Thing adds a heavyweight melee presence. Both heroes retain their comic‑book aesthetics, preserving the game’s nostalgic visual language. The addition expands team‑composition strategies, encouraging players to experiment with new synergies.

The update arrives amid a crowded indie beat‑em‑up market where fresh characters can extend a title’s lifespan. Early player feedback praises the balance of the new abilities, though some note that the game’s core mechanics remain unchanged.

## What to Watch Next

The CAR‑T HIV effort will move to a phase‑2 trial later this year; its enrollment numbers will indicate whether the approach can scale. Legal scrutiny of Norman’s training firm will likely intensify as victims’ families seek accountability. BMW’s M3 CS Handschalter will hit showrooms in early 2027, and sales figures will reveal if the manual gearbox justifies the niche positioning. Finally, Marvel plans a seasonal event that could introduce additional heroes, a move that will test whether roster expansions keep the beat‑em‑up community engaged.
