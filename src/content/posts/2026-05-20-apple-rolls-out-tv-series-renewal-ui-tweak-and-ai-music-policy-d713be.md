Apple announced three service updates this week: a second season for its culinary series, a new accessibility setting for Apple TV, and a policy brief on AI‑generated music.

Knife Edge: Chasing Michelin Stars will return for a second eight‑part season on Apple TV, after its first season earned critical praise. At the same time, Apple confirmed that tvOS 27, arriving next month, will add a Larger Text option for the Apple TV 4K. The streaming service also published an open letter titled “What We’re Doing to Keep Music Fair,” noting that AI‑generated tracks account for less than one percent of all plays.

## Knife Edge secures a second season on Apple TV

Apple TV confirmed the renewal of Knife Edge: Chasing Michelin Stars for a second eight‑episode run. The decision follows a debut season that critics praised for its behind‑the‑scenes look at Michelin‑starred kitchens.

The series fits Apple’s broader push into original nonfiction. By expanding its culinary catalog, Apple TV hopes to attract food‑enthusiast subscribers who already spend time on streaming platforms.

Apple has not disclosed a premiere date for season two. The lack of timing suggests the production schedule may still be fluid, leaving fans to wait for a formal announcement.

## tvOS 27 adds a Larger Text display option

Apple’s next major software release for the Apple TV 4K, tvOS 27, will ship a Larger Text setting. The feature enlarges on‑screen type without altering the underlying layout.

Larger Text targets users who find the default UI too small for distant viewing. The change works at the system level, so third‑party apps inherit the setting automatically.

Previous tvOS updates focused on performance and media formats. Introducing an accessibility‑oriented tweak signals Apple’s intent to tighten the user experience across its hardware.

## Apple Music outlines its AI fairness stance

Apple Music released an open letter titled “What We’re Doing to Keep Music Fair.” The note stresses that AI‑generated music makes up under one percent of total streams.

Industry observers worry that AI could erode songwriter royalties. Apple’s letter claims the company will monitor AI usage and keep a level playing field for creators.

The statement stops short of detailing enforcement mechanisms. Without clear metrics, the industry will watch how Apple translates policy into practice.

## Context: Apple’s service strategy under regulatory pressure

Apple’s simultaneous announcements reveal a coordinated effort to bolster its services ecosystem. Original content, UI refinements, and AI policy each address a different competitive front.

Regulators in the EU and the US are probing AI‑driven media for fairness and copyright compliance. Apple’s proactive letter may pre‑empt formal investigations.

Streaming rivals such as Netflix and Spotify have already rolled out accessibility features and AI guidelines. Apple’s moves aim to keep its services attractive while navigating emerging legal expectations.

## What to watch

Track the October rollout of tvOS 27 for user feedback on Larger Text. Watch for a release window for Knife Edge season two, which will indicate Apple’s commitment to niche nonfiction. Monitor Apple Music’s next transparency report to see if AI‑generated plays stay below the one‑percent threshold or if new compliance measures appear.
