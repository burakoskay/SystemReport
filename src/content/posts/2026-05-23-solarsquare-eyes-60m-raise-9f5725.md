## History of Rooftop Solar in India

The Indian government has been actively promoting renewable energy, particularly solar power, through various initiatives and policies. The Jawaharlal Nehru National Solar Mission (JNNSM) was launched in 2010 to promote solar energy in the country. The mission aimed to deploy 20,000 MW of solar power by 2022.

The government has also implemented various incentives, such as tax exemptions, subsidies, and low-interest loans, to encourage the adoption of solar energy. Additionally, the Indian Renewable Energy Development Agency (IREDA) has been providing financial assistance to renewable energy projects.

## Technical Mechanics of Rooftop Solar

Rooftop solar systems typically consist of solar panels, an inverter, and a mounting system. The solar panels convert sunlight into electricity, which is then inverted into alternating current (AC) electricity. The mounting system secures the solar panels to the rooftop.

The efficiency of rooftop solar systems depends on various factors, including the quality of the solar panels, the angle and orientation of the panels, and the amount of sunlight the panels receive.
