## Terraria adds cross‑play, teases 15th‑anniversary merch

Re‑Logic announced that cross‑play will arrive for *Terraria* after fifteen years of solo play. The studio did not disclose platforms or a launch window, but the confirmation ends long‑standing speculation among the community. Alongside the feature, Re‑Logic previewed a line of collector’s items to mark the game’s 15th anniversary. The items include enamel pins, a printed art book, and a limited‑edition cartridge‑style case. The studio highlighted that *Terraria* has sold 70 million copies over its fifteen‑year run, a figure that underscores the title’s staying power.

The cross‑play promise matters because the game’s sandbox nature thrives on multiplayer collaboration. Players on PC, consoles, and mobile will soon share the same world, removing the friction that has kept friends apart. The collector’s line targets long‑time fans who have built extensive bases and amassed rare gear. By tying the merchandise to a functional update, Re‑Logic hopes to convert nostalgia into renewed engagement.

## Polygon changes hands, staff cut

Vox Media sold Polygon to Valnet, a content‑aggregation company that runs sites such as Game Rant, OpenCritic, Android Police, and Comic Book Resources. Valnet reports over 260 million page views across its portfolio and is led by founder Hassan Youssef, who previously helped run Mansef Inc., the operator behind Brazzers and a former minority investor in Pornhub.

The sale triggered immediate layoffs at the 13‑year‑old outlet. Co‑founder and editor‑in‑chief Chris Plante, senior writer Michael McWhertor, senior reporter Nicole Carpenter, and several other veteran journalists were let go. Carpenter posted a story about the Epic vs. Apple case at 10 a.m. Eastern and announced her layoff less than two hours later on Bluesky. Co‑founder Brian Crecente wrote, “just completely sickened by this news.”

Other staff shared similar shock. Curation editor Pete Volk said, “Along with just about everyone else at Polygon, I am now out of a job, ending over a decade at Vox Media for me.” Entertainment reporter Petrana Radulovic posted, “I was just thinking earlier this week, ‘Man, I love my job and I’m so excited to jump into summer blockbuster season!’ And now I don’t have my job…”. Special projects editor Matt Leone added, “I really can’t complain too much—Polygon was a great place to work for the last decade‑plus—but if anyone’s hiring, please reach out!”

## Consolidation and platform convergence in gaming

The Polygon deal illustrates a broader trend of media consolidation. Large aggregators acquire niche outlets to capture audiences and advertising dollars. Valnet’s portfolio already spans dozens of brands, giving it leverage over ad rates and distribution channels. For journalists, the model often translates into lower pay and tighter editorial oversight, as reported by writers who claim Valnet compensates poorly for long‑form pieces. Valnet’s recent lawsuit against The Wrap, in which it defended its reputation for “high‑quality journalism,” signals a willingness to protect its brand aggressively.

At the same time, game developers are converging on cross‑play as a standard feature. Titles such as *Fortnite*, *Rocket League*, and *Minecraft* have long offered cross‑platform multiplayer, forcing competitors to follow suit or risk fragmentation. *Terraria*’s move aligns it with this ecosystem, potentially expanding its player base and extending the lifespan of its 15‑year title. The simultaneous emergence of cross‑play and media consolidation raises questions about how content creators and distributors will coordinate promotion, especially when ownership structures shift.

## What to watch

Re‑Logic has not set a release date for cross‑play, but community chatter suggests a rollout later this year. Tracking patch notes and platform‑specific announcements will reveal how quickly the feature spreads across PC, console, and mobile. On the media side, Polygon’s editorial direction under Valnet remains opaque. Watch for new bylines, changes to the site’s tone, and any further staff churn. The next hiring round, if any, will indicate whether Valnet intends to preserve Polygon’s reputation as a “gaming authority” or retool it to fit a broader content strategy. Both stories will shape how gamers discover, discuss, and play their favorite titles in a consolidating landscape.
