## Online Safety Gets an Open-Source Boost

Osprey 1.0, an open-source rules engine for online safety, processes over 50 million events daily in production environments like Bluesky. Developed originally by Discord and now open-sourced through ROOST, Osprey is designed to help online platforms investigate and address priority threats at scale.

The tool allows teams to encode and manage nuanced, platform-specific anti-abuse and safety rules, conduct investigations into emerging threats across systems, and support human reviewers with structured workflows. Osprey operates without sacrificing data privacy or performance.

## How Osprey Works

Osprey is designed to be flexible, extensible, and interoperable, integrating into existing architecture rather than replacing it. Its features support real-time processing capability, enabling teams to address emerging threats quickly. This flexibility is crucial for online platforms that need to adapt rapidly to new types of threats.
