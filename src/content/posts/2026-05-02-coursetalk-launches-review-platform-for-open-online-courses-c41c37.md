## CourseTalk Aims to Cut Through MOOC Noise

CourseTalk has launched to help learners find signal in the growing lineup of open online courses. The site allows students to share experiences with these courses and discover new ones. By providing a platform for reviews and ratings, CourseTalk aims to make it easier for learners to determine which courses are worth their time.

## The Problem of MOOC Noise

The rise of Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) has led to a surge in online learning resources. However, with many options available, it can be difficult for learners to determine which courses are worth their time. CourseTalk provides a Yelp-like platform for MOOCs, allowing learners to search for courses, browse top-rated and popular courses, and search by category.

## How CourseTalk Works

The platform currently supports courses from platforms like Coursera, Udacity, and edX, with plans to add more. CourseTalk also compiles a list of universities offering MOOCs and features top reviewers. This allows learners to see which courses are most popular and which reviewers are most trusted.

## A Meritocracy for Online Learning

The launch of CourseTalk comes as online learning continues to disrupt traditional education. A platform like CourseTalk could provide a valuable service by enabling a meritocracy, where high-quality courses can rise to the top. By allowing learners to share their experiences and rate courses, CourseTalk can help ensure that the best courses get the most attention.

## History of MOOCs

The concept of MOOCs has been around for several years. In 2012, Coursera was founded by two University of Michigan professors, and it quickly gained popularity. Other platforms, such as edX and Udacity, soon followed. Since then, MOOCs have become increasingly popular, with many universities and colleges offering their own courses.
