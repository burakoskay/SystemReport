## Introduction to OpenAI's Latest Moves
OpenAI co-founder Greg Brockman has taken charge of product strategy. The company plans to combine ChatGPT and Codex.

The move comes as OpenAI updates its Mac apps due to a security issue with a third-party developer tool. The company found no evidence of user data being accessed.

## Security Precautions
OpenAI is updating its security certifications for macOS applications. This requires all macOS users to update their OpenAI apps to the latest versions. The company identified a security issue involving a third-party developer tool, Axios, which was part of a broader industry incident.

OpenAI's security precautions are a response to the potential risk of someone attempting to distribute a fake app that appears to be from OpenAI. The company has provided links to its four Mac apps: ChatGPT, Codex, Atlas, and Codex CLI.

After May 8, older versions of these apps may no longer work. OpenAI's decision to update its security certifications is a precautionary measure to protect its users.

## Mental Health Concerns
OpenAI has released estimates of ChatGPT users who may show signs of severe mental health crises. The company worked with experts to update the chatbot to recognize indicators of mental distress and guide users toward real-world support.

Around 0.07 percent of active ChatGPT users show possible signs of mental health emergencies related to psychosis or mania. Another 0.15 percent have conversations that include explicit indicators of potential suicidal planning or intent.

OpenAI's estimates suggest that every seven days, around 560,000 people may be exchanging messages with ChatGPT that indicate they are experiencing mania or psychosis. About 1.2 million more are possibly expressing suicidal ideations.

## Technical Improvements
OpenAI's latest version of GPT-5 is designed to express empathy while avoiding affirming beliefs that don’t have basis in reality. The company worked with over 170 medical experts to review more than 1,800 model responses involving potential psychosis, suicide, and emotional attachment.

The clinicians found that the newer model reduced undesired answers between 39 percent and 52 percent across all categories. OpenAI's technical improvements aim to provide more reliable support for users experiencing mental health emergencies.
