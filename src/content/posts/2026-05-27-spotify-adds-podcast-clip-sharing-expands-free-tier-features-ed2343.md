# Spotify adds podcast clip sharing, expands free-tier features

Spotify now lets users save or share a specific moment from a podcast episode with a simple trimming tool. The feature, rolling out to Free and Premium users, allows listeners to highlight moments without leaving the app.

## Clip sharing arrives on Spotify

The new button sits beside the usual save-episode and add-to-playlist icons. Tapping it opens a slider that lets you mark a start and end point. Once you hit "Create link," the app copies a short URL to the clipboard. The feature also allows users to save clips locally.

The rollout starts on iOS and Android and spreads to the desktop client over the next few weeks. No subscription tier is required, which mirrors the company’s historic push to keep the free experience robust.

## Competitive context: YouTube, Apple, Amazon

The feature arrives just weeks after YouTube pulled its own Clips function. Apple recently added a "Share Clip" button in its Podcasts app. Google’s YouTube Music lets users copy timestamps. This move by Spotify puts pressure on competitors to enhance their own podcast sharing capabilities.

## History of podcast sharing features

In the past, podcast sharing has been limited to sharing entire episodes. Spotify's new feature is a significant step forward in allowing users to share specific moments from podcast episodes. This feature has been a long time coming, and its introduction will likely change the way users interact with podcast content.

## Technical mechanics and user experience

The trimming tool offers a simple UI; users can define a start and end point. The app records the timestamps and generates a short token that maps to the original episode URL plus the offset parameters. When a recipient clicks the link, the player seeks to the start timestamp and begins playback.

The feature integrates with Spotify’s existing sharing stack. Users can push the link to Instagram Stories, Twitter, or embed it in a blog post. Because the link resolves inside the Spotify app, it drives additional session time.
