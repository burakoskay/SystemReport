## Understanding Hacker News Trends

Hacker News, a platform for tech enthusiasts and professionals, has a front page that showcases the most popular articles. But have you ever wondered what makes an article successful on this platform? A recent analysis of Hacker News submissions reveals some interesting trends.

The analysis found that stories submitted on weekends are nearly 50% more likely to make it to the front page. This contradicts the conventional wisdom that weekday mornings, specifically 5AM PST, are the best time to submit articles.

## Diving Deeper into Submission Trends

The study looked at the historical data of Hacker News submissions to determine the best time to post. It defined a score threshold of 10 as a heuristic for "success," which roughly translates to making it to the front page. The results showed that weekend submissions have a higher success rate.

Moreover, the average scores of submissions, which are likely related to traffic, also suggest that weekends are the better time to submit a story. While it's possible that the ratio of views to votes changes on weekends, the data suggests that submitting on weekends can increase your chances of getting on the front page.

## Patterns in a 24-Hour Period

When looking at the patterns over a 24-hour period, there are peaks at around 6:00AM, 10:30AM, and 5:00PM PST. These peaks could be attributed to a mixture of before/after work and lunch times in different time zones.

## The Role of Buzzwords in Article Success

The analysis also explored the impact of buzzwords on article success. Using a Bag of Words/Naive Bayes approach, it was found that certain words, such as "TensorFlow," can increase an article's chances of success. However, the study also notes that blindly upvoting articles with popular words may not be the best strategy.
