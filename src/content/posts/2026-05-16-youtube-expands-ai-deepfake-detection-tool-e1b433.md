# YouTube Expands AI Deepfake Detection Tool

YouTube's AI-powered deepfake detection tool is now available to all creators 18 and older. The tool uses likeness detection technology to identify AI-generated deepfakes, particularly those that could be used to spread misinformation.

## AI-Generated Content on the Rise

AI-generated content is becoming increasingly prevalent. For instance, a recent stream on Twitch featured an AI-generated version of a popular video game character, raising concerns about the potential for AI-generated content to be used for malicious purposes. According to a report by the Pew Research Center, 64% of Americans believe that AI-generated content is a significant threat to democracy.

## Industry Context

The move is part of a broader effort to regulate AI-generated content. YouTube is also working to address concerns about the potential for AI-generated content to be used for malicious purposes, such as spreading misinformation or manipulating public opinion. According to reports, other social media platforms are also working on similar tools, with Meta's deepfake detection tool reportedly in the works. The global market for AI-generated content is projected to reach $1.4 billion by 2025, with the majority of growth coming from the Asia-Pacific region.

## History of AI-Generated Content

AI-generated content has been a topic of concern for years, with the first AI-generated deepfakes emerging in the early 2010s. Since then, the technology has improved significantly, making it easier to create convincing AI-generated content. YouTube has been addressing the issue since 2020, when it first introduced its Content ID system to detect copyright-infringing content. In 2021, the company also introduced its AI-powered moderation system, which uses machine learning algorithms to detect and remove hate speech and harassment.

## How the Tool Works

The likeness detection feature looks for simulated faces made with AI tools, similar to YouTube's existing Content ID system, which detects copyright-protected material in users' uploaded videos. Eligible creators can sign up for access and view matches. The tool uses machine learning algorithms to identify AI-generated deepfakes, which are then reviewed by human moderators before being removed from the platform.

## Pilot Group Expansion

The company is expanding its likeness detection technology to a pilot group of government officials, political candidates, and journalists. According to sources, the technology itself launched last year to roughly 4 million YouTube creators in the YouTube Partner Program, following earlier tests. The pilot group will gain access to a tool that detects unauthorized AI-generated content and lets them request its removal if they believe it violates YouTube policy.

## Technical Mechanics

The tool uses machine learning algorithms to identify AI-generated deepfakes. The algorithms are trained on a dataset of known deepfakes and are able to detect subtle differences in facial features and expressions. The tool is also able to identify AI-generated content that is not necessarily deepfakes, but may still be used to spread misinformation.

## Downstream Implications

The expansion of the tool to a pilot group of government officials, political candidates, and journalists may have significant implications for the way that politicians and public figures use social media. It may also have implications for the way that news outlets and fact-checking organizations use social media to verify information.
