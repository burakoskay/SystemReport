## Understanding Hacker News Trends

Hacker News, a popular platform for tech enthusiasts and professionals, has long been a hub for discussing the latest developments in technology, entrepreneurship, and innovation. With thousands of submissions daily, understanding what makes an article successful can be crucial for content creators. A recent analysis dives into the historical data of Hacker News submissions to uncover trends and insights that could help authors increase their article's visibility.

## The Data-Driven Approach

The analysis focused on the success of articles, defined by their ability to reach the front page, indicated by a score threshold of 10. This heuristic was chosen as a reasonable approximation of making it to the front page, where articles can reach a broader audience. The study revealed some surprising insights: stories submitted on weekends are nearly 50% more likely to make it to the front page compared to those submitted on weekdays.

## Unconventional Wisdom

Contrary to the conventional wisdom that submitting articles on weekday mornings, specifically around 5AM PST, increases their chances of reaching the front page, the data suggests that weekends might offer a better window for submissions. This finding was further supported by examining the average scores of submissions over time, which showed a similar pattern. The weekend boost in success rates and scores implies that the dynamics of user engagement and voting behavior differ significantly from weekdays.

## Patterns and Peaks

Zooming into the daily patterns, the analysis identified peaks in article success around 6:00AM, 10:30AM, and 5:00PM PST. These times correspond to typical work and lunch hours across different time zones, suggesting that the timing of submissions could coincide with users' habits of checking and voting on articles. Understanding these patterns can help authors strategically plan their submissions.

## The Role of Buzzwords

The study also explored the impact of buzzwords, such as "TensorFlow," on article success. Using a Bag of Words/Naive Bayes approach, the analysis found that the presence of certain keywords could indeed influence an article's popularity. However, the effect might not be as straightforward as blindly upvoting articles with trendy terms, as the relationship between keywords and success is complex and influenced by multiple factors.

## Classifying Articles

Another aspect of the analysis involved classifying Hacker News articles to produce a purified feed. This was achieved by developing a classifier that looks at tokens in article titles and uses logistic regression to predict article success. The classifier's performance was evaluated by comparing its predictions to manual evaluations, providing insights into the factors that contribute to an article's success.

## What's Next

For those interested in maximizing their article's visibility on Hacker News, the findings suggest reconsidering the timing of submissions, with weekends potentially offering a higher chance of success. Additionally, understanding the impact of keywords and the daily patterns of user engagement can help authors strategize their submissions more effectively.
