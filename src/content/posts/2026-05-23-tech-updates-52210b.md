## iOS Updates

Apple has been warning owners of older iPhones and iPads that they need to update their devices to the latest iOS software in order to maintain accurate GPS location and continue using the App Store, iCloud, email, and web browsing. This is due to the GPS time rollover issue, which occurs every 19 years when GPS devices need to reset in order to accurately measure time and dates.

According to Apple, iPhone and iPad products from 2012 and earlier will need to be updated to iOS 10.3.4 or 9.3.6, depending on the device. If users don't update their devices, they won't be able to connect to the internet. This is a significant issue, as it will affect many users who still use older Apple devices.

The GPS time rollover issue is a technical problem that arises from the way GPS devices calculate time. GPS devices use a 10-bit counter to calculate time, which wraps around every 19 years. When this counter wraps around, GPS devices need to reset to avoid errors. Apple's update addresses this issue by ensuring that older devices can accurately calculate time and dates.
