Sam Altman's World, co-founder of OpenAI, has introduced an identity-verifying orb that has made its way to Tinder. This orb, which uses facial scanning technology, requires users to physically visit one of World's locations to prove they're human. The reward? Five free boosts on the popular dating app.

But verification isn't the only area where tech is pushing boundaries. Prediction market exchanges, such as Polymarket and Kalshi, have created platforms where users can wager on real-world events. This raises questions about the role of journalists and the ethics of monetizing information. Can these platforms be trusted to provide accurate information, or do they pose a threat to traditional media?
