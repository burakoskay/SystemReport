## The Update Targets Content Aggregators

Instagram has taken a hard stance against content aggregators that re-upload others' posts without adding significant value. The platform's recommendation algorithm will now penalize 'unoriginal' photo and carousel posts.

## Meme Creators and Aggregator Accounts at Risk

According to the sources, meme creators and others who don't make 'material' edits to the content they share could see their reach reduced. Instagram's update specifically targets accounts that don't post original content and instead simply re-upload others' posts.

## Industry Context and History

The move to crack down on content aggregators is not unprecedented. Instagram's update reflects the platform's aim to promote original content creators. The update targets specific types of posts, such as photo and carousel posts, that lack original content. Instagram's parent company, Meta, has faced criticism for promoting content that spreads misinformation and hate speech, leading to increased scrutiny of the platform's algorithm.

The industry context is also shaped by the growing competition from TikTok and other short-form video platforms. These platforms have attracted a younger demographic, forcing Instagram to adapt its content strategy to remain competitive. The update is part of Instagram's broader effort to shift its focus towards more interactive and engaging content.

## Technical Mechanics of the Algorithm Update

The new algorithm update will assess the content posted by users and penalize those who don't add significant value to the original content. Instagram's policy states that 'material' edits require more than just re-uploading content. This is achieved through a combination of natural language processing and machine learning algorithms that analyze the content's uniqueness, engagement, and overall value.

## Downstream Implications

As Instagram's algorithm update takes effect, users can expect to see a shift in the type of content that appears on their feeds. Content aggregators and meme creators will need to adapt to the new algorithm by focusing on creating original content or making significant edits to the content they share. This may lead to a decline in reach and engagement for these types of accounts, potentially forcing them to reconsider their content strategy.

On the other hand, original content creators may see an increase in visibility and engagement, as the algorithm prioritizes unique and valuable content. This shift in algorithmic priorities may also lead to a more diverse and dynamic content ecosystem, as creators are incentivized to produce high-quality, engaging content.
