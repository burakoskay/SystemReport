## Meta's AI-Powered Age Verification

Meta has started using AI to analyze users' facial features to identify if they are underage. The system, which is currently operating in select countries, uses visual analysis to determine if a user's age appears to be under 13. This technology aims to enhance Meta's age verification processes, which have been a point of contention for the company in the past. For instance, accurate age verification is crucial for ensuring that minors are not exposed to content or features intended for older users.

## Technical Mechanics

The AI-powered system assesses users' facial features, including height and bone structure, to estimate their age. This method is based on the idea that certain physical characteristics can be correlated with age. For instance, bone structure and facial proportions change significantly during childhood and adolescence. By analyzing these features, the AI can make an educated estimate about a user's age. The technology leverages machine learning algorithms that are trained on large datasets of facial images, allowing it to improve its accuracy over time. Meta says it's working toward a broader rollout of the technology, which implies that the company is refining the system and preparing it for use in more countries.

## History of Age Verification Efforts

Meta has faced challenges with age verification in the past. In 2019, the company was fined $5 billion by the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) for violating a 2012 consent decree related to user privacy. One of the issues was the company's failure to adequately verify users' ages, which led to underage users being able to access content and features intended for older users. Since then, Meta has been working to improve its age verification processes, including the use of AI-powered systems like the one launched recently. This history underscores the importance of robust age verification mechanisms and the need for continuous improvement in this area.
