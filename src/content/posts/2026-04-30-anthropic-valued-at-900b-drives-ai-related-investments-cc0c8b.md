## The Subsea Cable Boom

Over 95% of international data and voice traffic travels through a near-million-mile web of underwater cables. Industry veterans point to a surge in demand as web-scale players support compute-intensive AI models. The increasing reliance on AI and the need for rapid data exchange have created a new wave of investment in subsea cable infrastructure.

The history of subsea cables dates back to 1850, when the first commercial telecommunication subsea cable was laid across the English Channel. Since then, the technology has evolved from telegraphs to coaxial cables and, most recently, to fiber optics. Today, these cables carry government communications, financial transactions, email, video calls, and streaming around the world.

## Technical Mechanics

The subsea cables used today are fiber optic cables that transmit data as light signals through thin glass or plastic fibers. The cables are designed to withstand harsh underwater conditions and are typically laid on the ocean floor by specialized vessels. The process of laying these cables involves careful planning and execution to ensure reliable data transmission.

The demand for subsea cables is driven by the growing need for data exchange between data centers, cloud providers, and network operators. As AI models become more complex and require more data to train, the need for high-capacity subsea cables will continue to grow.
