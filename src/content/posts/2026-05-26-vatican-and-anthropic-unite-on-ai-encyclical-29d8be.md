## Pope Leo XIV Launches AI Encyclical with Anthropic Co-Founder
Pope Leo XIV, born in Chicago, unveiled *Magnifica Humanitas*, his first encyclical and major text of his papacy, framing AI as a moral question. The document emphasizes the protection of the human person in the age of artificial intelligence and condemns the concentration of technological power in a handful of global players. This move signals the Vatican's proactive stance on AI ethics.

## Stakes for Workers, Warfare, and Corporate Power
The encyclical highlights AI as a threat to human dignity. It signals a shift from abstract caution to concrete policy proposals. The Vatican's engagement with multiple tech firms suggests a strategy of diversifying input rather than aligning with any single corporate agenda. This approach may influence AI development trajectories and regulations.

## Historical Echo: From the Industrial Revolution to the AI Age
Leo's encyclical mirrors Pope Leo XIII's 1891 *Rerum Novarum*, a response to the Industrial Revolution's impact on workers and capitalism. The historical parallel highlights a shift in presentation style, with lay experts sharing the podium, acknowledging technical expertise outside the clerical hierarchy. This evolution reflects a more inclusive approach to addressing complex societal challenges.
