## Google Takes Aim at Fake Reviews

Google has updated its review policies to ban people from posting negative reviews of their former employers on its business tool, Google My Business. The move aims to make reviews more accurate and honest, according to the company. This change comes as Google tries to clamp down on who can post reviews. The company considers posting negative content about a current or former employment experience to be a "conflict of interest." Users are also banned from reviewing their own business and posting negative content about a competitor to manipulate ratings.

## A History of Review Manipulation

This isn't the first time companies have tried to game the review system. Earlier this month, school children in Europe flooded Google Classroom with one-star reviews in an effort to force it offline. The tactic was first used by students in China, who spammed the Alibaba-owned app DingTalk with bad reviews. This incident highlights the challenges companies face in maintaining the integrity of their review systems. In another instance, students under coronavirus quarantine in China used the same tactic to try and knock Google Classroom offline.
