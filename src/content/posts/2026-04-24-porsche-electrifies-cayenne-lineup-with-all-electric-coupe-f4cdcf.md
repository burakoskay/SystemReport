## Porsche's Electric Cayenne Coupe Debuts This Summer

Porsche is launching an all-electric version of its popular Cayenne SUV, specifically designed as a coupe, later this summer. This move marks a significant step in the company's electrification strategy, joining the growing list of luxury automakers shifting towards battery-powered vehicles. The Cayenne Coupe Electric is set to start selling in late summer, as announced by Porsche.

## A Growing Trend in Electric Vehicle Manufacturing

The introduction of the electric Cayenne coupe comes as the automotive industry continues to embrace electric vehicle (EV) technology. A key trend in this shift is the use of giga-castings, large structural components that integrate multiple parts into a single ultra-large casting. Pioneered by Tesla for its Model Y, giga-castings have reduced the number of parts, eliminated welds, and streamlined manufacturing. Other manufacturers, including Volvo and Polestar, have also adopted this technology. Giga-castings are changing the way cars are made, making the process more efficient and cost-effective.

## Impact on Aluminum Content and Manufacturing

The increasing use of giga-castings could significantly impact aluminum content in vehicles. Traditionally, aluminum usage in light vehicles has been growing, with castings being the predominant form. However, with the shift towards giga-castings, the product mix is changing. Aluminum growth is now shifting towards the car body, chassis, battery trays, and electric drive components. According to a study by Viami International Inc., aluminum usage in light vehicles has surpassed 500 lbs (227 kg) per light vehicle in North America and 396 lbs (180 kg) per vehicle in Europe. While higher aluminum content means higher costs and a larger carbon footprint, manufacturers are working to lower material and processing costs while improving sustainability.

## Wireless Charging on the Go

In related news, researchers at Auckland University have been studying the feasibility of electric vehicle charging on the go, using wireless charging pads embedded in the road. Their study found that dynamic wireless charging facilities could be more cost-effective than traditional plug-in charging stations, especially at higher inductive power transfer capacities. As the population of EVs increases, the value of time saved with wireless charging will become more significant, making it a competitive option. The study developed a traffic simulation framework and a "state of charge" model to evaluate the effectiveness of wireless charging.
